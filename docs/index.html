01The premise

A transition pathway, not a degree

Your maintenance years are the asset. What's missing is specific and closable: how things are actually made, the quantitative reliability toolkit, and the planning system vocabulary. This site closes those gaps in a deliberate order and then tells you exactly which doors in Kuwait to knock on.

Everything here is taught the way a professor teaches a serious adult student — from the international textbooks this discipline actually uses (Groover, Kalpakjian & Schmid, Callister, Çengel, Flemings), with every equation derived or explicitly sourced, and every industrial example drawn from six real Kuwait manufacturers: Kuwait Steel, Gulf Cable, Kirby Building Systems, EQUATE, KDD / Petra, and HEISCO. No invented plant data — representative figures are labeled as exactly that.

02How the content is built

Three depths, honestly labeled

Full lectures

The gold standard: complete derivations, worked examples with units carried through, misconceptions addressed, a check-your-understanding problem, and a Kuwait floor section. The two casting lectures are built; more follow.

Badge: FULL LECTURE

Core 60 study guides

The sixty lessons that interviews and plant floors actually test — processes, fundamentals, reliability, condition monitoring, rotating equipment, planning, automation. Compact guides: objectives, sourced equations, one worked example, misconceptions, a Kuwait line.

Badge: STUDY GUIDE (queued ones are labeled)

Structured notes

Every remaining lesson in all 48 courses carries a precise scope note and the textbook it is taught from — so the full map is always visible, and nothing pretends to be deeper than it is.

03Where to start

Two doors in

If you want the teaching: start with Manufacturing Processes I — its two casting lectures are the site's gold standard, and its study guides cover the processes Kuwait's plants actually run. Then follow the Core 60 through the full curriculum map.

If you want the job: read the career playbook first — CV translation, the six target employers, certifications in the right order, the interview map, and the 12-month plan. It will tell you which lessons to study first and why.
