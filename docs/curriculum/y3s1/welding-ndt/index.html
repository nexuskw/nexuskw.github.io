WLD 305Year 3 · Semester 1

Welding Engineering & NDT

The joint as an engineered product: weld metallurgy and the HAZ, defects and their acceptance, procedure qualification (WPS/PQR), and the NDT methods — PT, MT, UT, RT — that decide what ships. Kirby and HEISCO territory.

12 lessons · 3 in the Core 60 · Primary texts:

  • Groover, Fundamentals of Modern Manufacturing — joining chapters
  • Kalpakjian & Schmid, Manufacturing Engineering and Technology
  • Callister & Rethwisch — phase transformation chapters
01

Welding as a metallurgical event

The weld thermal cycle: peak temperatures and cooling rates across the joint. Fusion zone, HAZ, and base metal as three different materials. Why welding is heat treatment you didn't order.

Taught from — Groover, welding chapters; Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 10

02

Weld metallurgy and the HAZCore 60 · guide queued

HAZ sub-zones mapped to the Fe–C diagram; hardening, softening, and grain growth located. Carbon equivalent, preheat, and hydrogen control as the cold-cracking triangle. The study guide every welding interview draws from.

Taught from — CORE 60 · A13 · Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 9–10; Groover, welding chapters

03

Residual stress and distortion

Why welds pull: thermal contraction against restraint. Distortion modes and sequencing countermeasures. PWHT — what it relieves and what it costs.

Taught from — Kalpakjian & Schmid, joining chapters

04

Weld defects and discontinuitiesCore 60 · guide queued

Porosity, lack of fusion, undercut, cracks hot and cold, inclusions — each with its cause chain and its radiographic/ultrasonic signature. Discontinuity versus defect: acceptance criteria as the deciding line. WPS/PQR logic: qualifying a procedure, then policing it.

Taught from — CORE 60 · A14 · Groover, welding chapters; Kalpakjian & Schmid

05

Welding symbols and joint design review

Symbol fluency on fabrication drawings; effective throat and size selection recalled from design. Access and position as quality factors. Reading a Kirby-style shop drawing's weld map.

Taught from — Groover; Kalpakjian & Schmid

06

Welding of specific materials

Carbon and low-alloy steels, stainless (sensitization and ferrite control), and aluminum's oxide and porosity problems. Dissimilar joints in outline. Matching filler to base with reasons.

Taught from — Kalpakjian & Schmid; Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 11

07

NDT principles and the methods matrixCore 60 · guide queued

PT, MT, UT, and RT: physical principle, what each detects, surface versus volumetric reach, and access/geometry limits — condensed into the selection matrix inspectors actually use. Personnel qualification levels demystified. Which method finds which weld defect, and why.

Taught from — CORE 60 · A15 · Groover, inspection chapters; Kalpakjian & Schmid

08

Ultrasonic testing in depth

Wave propagation, probes, and calibration blocks. Angle-beam weld scanning geometry. Sizing and characterizing indications — where UT skill actually lives.

Taught from — Kalpakjian & Schmid, inspection sections

09

Radiographic testing in depth

Source selection, geometry, and image quality indicators. Film and digital reading of the classic defect appearances. Radiation safety as an absolute boundary.

Taught from — Kalpakjian & Schmid, inspection sections

10

Welding procedure and welder qualification

WPS variables — essential and nonessential; PQR testing: tensiles, bends, macros. Welder performance qualification and its limits of position and thickness. The paperwork that makes welds trustworthy.

Taught from — Groover; Kalpakjian & Schmid

11

Cutting, gouging, and repair welding

Arc gouging and defect excavation; repair sequences that don't make things worse. Excavation verification by MT/PT. Repair welding as controlled surgery, permitted and documented.

Taught from — Kalpakjian & Schmid

12

Fabrication quality plan project

Assemble an inspection and test plan for a welded assembly: hold points, NDT extents, acceptance criteria, and records. The QA/QC document flow of a fabrication contract walked once end to end.

Taught from — Kalpakjian & Schmid; Groover
