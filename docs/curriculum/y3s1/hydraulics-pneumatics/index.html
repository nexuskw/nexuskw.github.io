HYD 304Year 3 · Semester 1

Hydraulics & Pneumatics

Fluid power for people who will maintain it: pumps, valves, actuators, and circuits read from schematics; pressure, flow, and force sized; and the contamination control that decides hydraulic life.

11 lessons · 0 in the Core 60 · Primary texts:

  • NPTEL: Fundamentals of Industrial Oil Hydraulics and Pneumatics (nptel.ac.in)
01

Fluid power principles

Pascal's law to force multiplication; power as pressure times flow. Where fluid power beats electromechanical drives. The efficiency price paid for force density.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Oil Hydraulics and Pneumatics

02

Hydraulic fluids and contamination control

Fluid functions and properties; ISO cleanliness codes read and specified. Filtration placement and beta ratios. Contamination as the leading cause of hydraulic failure — treated with due respect.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Oil Hydraulics and Pneumatics

03

Hydraulic pumps

Gear, vane, and piston pumps; fixed versus variable displacement. Volumetric efficiency measured and trended. Pressure-compensated control explained without mystery.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Oil Hydraulics and Pneumatics

04

Actuators

Cylinder sizing for force and speed; cushioning and seal packages. Hydraulic motors in brief. Rod buckling and mounting classes — statics put to work.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Oil Hydraulics and Pneumatics

05

Pressure and flow control valves

Relief, reducing, and sequence valves; flow controls and meter-in/meter-out logic. Cracking pressure and override characterized. The valves that shape a circuit's personality.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Oil Hydraulics and Pneumatics

06

Directional valves and circuit reading

Spool centers and their consequences; ISO 1219 symbols read fluently. Standard circuits: regenerative, counterbalance, unloading. Schematic-to-machine correspondence drilled.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Oil Hydraulics and Pneumatics

07

Accumulators and system dynamics

Gas-charged accumulators: sizing, precharge, and safety. Shock suppression and supplementary flow. Stored energy respected during maintenance.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Oil Hydraulics and Pneumatics

08

Hydraulic system troubleshooting

Heat, noise, and slow motion as the three complaints; a diagnostic tree for each. Test-point measurements against the schematic. Case-based practice on press and mobile circuits.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Oil Hydraulics and Pneumatics

09

Pneumatic systems

Air preparation — filter, regulator, lubricator; valve and cylinder families; sizing with compressibility acknowledged. Where pneumatics wins: speed, cleanliness, simplicity. Leakage economics revisited from Thermo II.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Oil Hydraulics and Pneumatics

10

Electro-pneumatics and machine motion

Solenoid valves, reed sensors, and PLC-driven pneumatic sequences. Grippers and pick-and-place motions. The actuation layer of packaging machinery.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Oil Hydraulics and Pneumatics

11

Proportional and servo systems first contact

Proportional valves and closed-loop positioning in outline. Where servo-hydraulics still rules: presses and test rigs. The control-systems course connected to oil.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Oil Hydraulics and Pneumatics
