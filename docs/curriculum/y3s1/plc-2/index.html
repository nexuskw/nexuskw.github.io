PLC II & Industrial Networks

Beyond single-rack thinking: structured programming, analog and PID in the PLC, fieldbuses and industrial Ethernet, drives integration, and the networked architecture of a modern line.

01

Structured programming and IEC languages

Function blocks, structured text, and sequential function charts beside ladder. Choosing a language per task. Code organization for machines with modes.

State machines for sequential control

Modes, states, and transitions designed before coding. Auto/manual/maintenance mode architecture. The pattern that makes complex machines debuggable.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Automation and Control

03

Analog processing and PID in the PLC

Scaling, filtering, and alarm limits on analog channels. The PLC PID instruction configured and tuned. Temperature and flow loops closed in logic.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Automation and Control

04

Industrial networks I: fieldbus fundamentals

Why networks replaced point wiring; topology, addressing, and determinism. Modbus RTU/TCP as the lingua franca. Reading a network architecture drawing.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Automation and Control

05

Industrial networks II: industrial Ethernet

EtherNet/IP and PROFINET in outline; switches, VLANs, and segmentation for machine networks. Diagnostics from port LEDs to managed-switch counters. When the 'PLC fault' is a network fault.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Automation and Control

06

VFDs and motion integration

Drive parameters, control words, and status words over the network. Speed and torque references from logic. Commissioning a networked drive without folklore.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Automation and Control

07

Data handling and recipes

Arrays, structures, and recipe management for product changeovers. Batch parameters downloaded safely. Production data captured at source for OEE.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Automation and Control

08

Alarm management and diagnostics design

Alarm philosophy applied in logic: priority, deadband, and state-based suppression. First-out capture for trips. Designing the diagnostics you will want during the outage.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Automation and Control

09

OT cybersecurity basics

Why plant networks are attacked and how they are segmented, patched, and backed up. Remote access done with adult supervision. The maintenance engineer's cyber hygiene responsibilities.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Automation and Control

10

Integration project: a two-station line

Two PLC stations, one network, shared interlocks, one HMI: specify, program, and fault-test the pair. Handshake design under failure conditions. The systems view PLC I could not yet teach.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Automation and Control
