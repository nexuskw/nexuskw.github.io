MDN 301Year 3 · Semester 1

Machine Design II — Machine Elements

The rotating-equipment bill of materials designed properly: gears, rolling and journal bearings, belts and chains, clutches, brakes, and couplings — every element a maintenance engineer has replaced, now sized from first principles.

11 lessons · 0 in the Core 60 · Primary texts:

  • NPTEL: Machine Design (nptel.ac.in)
  • MIT OCW 2.72 Elements of Mechanical Design
01

Spur gear design: bending

Lewis bending analysis with velocity and geometry factors. Module and face width chosen against tooth breakage. Where root cracks start and why.

Taught from — NPTEL: Machine Design

02

Spur gear design: surface durability

Contact stress by Hertzian logic; pitting as the surface fatigue mode. Bending versus pitting as competing failure budgets. The design meaning of gear micropitting found in an oil sample.

Taught from — NPTEL: Machine Design

03

Helical, bevel, and worm gearing

Helical geometry and axial thrust; bevel basics; worm-drive efficiency and its heat problem. Selection logic across gear types. Thrust-bearing consequences of helix choices.

Taught from — NPTEL: Machine Design

04

Rolling-element bearings

Bearing types and the L10 life equation used honestly. Equivalent loads with combined radial and thrust. Fits, preload, and mounting — where installed life is won or lost.

Taught from — NPTEL: Machine Design

05

Journal bearings and lubrication theory

Hydrodynamic action from Reynolds-equation reasoning in outline; Sommerfeld thinking and minimum film thickness. Viscosity selection and clearance. The bearings under every large turbine and compressor.

Taught from — NPTEL: Machine Design

06

Belt drives

V-belt mechanics from the capstan relation; power ratings and service factors. Tensioning — the maintenance variable that decides belt life. Multi-belt sets and matching.

Taught from — NPTEL: Machine Design

07

Chain drives

Roller-chain geometry and chordal action. Ratings, lubrication regimes, and elongation limits. Sprocket wear read as a diagnostic.

Taught from — NPTEL: Machine Design

08

Clutches and brakes

Friction clutch and brake torque from pressure assumptions — uniform wear versus uniform pressure derived. Energy dissipation and thermal sizing. Where brakes fade and clutches slip.

Taught from — NPTEL: Machine Design

09

Couplings and shaft alignment tolerances

Rigid, elastomeric, gear, and disc couplings; what misalignment each tolerates and transmits. Coupling selection against torque and misalignment spectra. The design basis under alignment standards used in the field.

Taught from — NPTEL: Machine Design

10

Flywheels and rotating-part integrity

Rotating-disc stresses and burst margins. Flywheel energy sizing revisited with stress limits. Overspeed as a design case, not an accident report.

Taught from — NPTEL: Machine Design

11

Design review: a gearbox teardown on paper

A complete gearbox audited element by element: gears, bearings, shafts, seals, housing. Each element's failure budget identified. The design-side twin of a maintenance teardown report.

Taught from — NPTEL: Machine Design
