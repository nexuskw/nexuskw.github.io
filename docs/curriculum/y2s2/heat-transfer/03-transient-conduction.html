01 · Lumped capacitance, and its entry ticket

If a body's own internal conduction is fast enough that it stays at one uniform temperature while it heats or cools, an energy balance on the whole body — stored energy lost to convection, \(\rho V c\,dT/dt=-hA(T-T_\infty)\) — integrates directly to \(\dfrac{T-T_\infty}{T_i-T_\infty}=e^{-t/\tau}\), a single exponential set by one time constant \(\tau=\rho V c/(hA)\). The entry ticket for this shortcut is the Biot number, \(Bi=hL_c/k\) with \(L_c=V/A\): it is a ratio of internal conduction resistance \((L_c/k)\) to external convection resistance \((1/h)\), and the common rule of thumb is \(Bi<0.1\) — internal resistance small enough next to the external bottleneck that "uniform temperature" is a good approximation, not merely a convenient one.

02 · When lumped fails: the semi-infinite solid and one-term charts

When \(Bi\) is not small — a thick section, a highly conductive-but-large body, or simply the first instants after a sudden surface change, before the interior has had time to feel it — a body is better modeled as a semi-infinite solid: an error-function temperature profile spreading in from the surface, whose surface heat flux falls as \(1/\sqrt{t}\), because the depth the disturbance has reached keeps growing while the local gradient keeps relaxing. For finite shapes (a plane wall, a long cylinder, a sphere) once enough time has passed that a single mode dominates (\(Fo=\alpha t/L_c^2\gtrsim0.2\)), a one-term solution \(\theta^*=C_1 e^{-\lambda_1^2 Fo}\) (times a position function), with \(\lambda_1\) and \(C_1\) tabulated against \(Bi\) and geometry, replaces the full infinite series — the same role Heisler charts play by hand.

03 · The same V/A ratio, a different exponent

Both \(\tau=\rho V c/(hA)\) here and Chvorinov's rule for casting solidification time (\(t\propto(V/A)^2\), covered in this curriculum's manufacturing-processes course) are governed by the same geometric ratio, \(V/A\) — a body's volume against the surface it must lose or gain heat through. The exponent differs because the limiting resistance differs: lumped capacitance's \(\tau\) is externally convection-limited, and \(V/A\) enters linearly through the energy balance; Chvorinov's rule describes a growing solid shell whose own conduction resistance is what's controlling, and that internally-limited problem is what pushes the exponent to two. Estimating a real time honestly means checking \(Bi\) first and picking the model that ticket actually admits — never assuming lumped capacitance applies just because it is the easier formula to reach for.

04 · A worked quench, checked before it is trusted

A 12 mm-diameter steel pin (\(k=45\ \text{W/mK}\), \(\rho=7850\ \text{kg/m}^3\), \(c=470\ \text{J/kgK}\)) is quenched in agitated oil, \(h=750\ \text{W/m}^2\text{K}\). For a long cylinder, \(L_c=V/A=r/2=3.0\ \ \text{mm}\), so \(Bi=hL_c/k=0.05\) — comfortably under 0.1, lumped capacitance is legitimate. \(\tau=\rho L_c c/h=14.76\ \text{s}\): physically, the time for the excess temperature to fall to \(1/e\approx37\%\) of its start. Reaching 5% of the initial excess takes \(t=\tau\ln(20)=44.2\ \text{s}\); reaching 10% takes \(t=\tau\ln(10)=34.0\ \ \text{s}\). Had the same pin instead been quenched in water (\(h=5000\ \text{W/m}^2\text{K}\)), \(Bi\) would climb to 0.33 — over the 0.1 line, meaning the lumped shortcut is no longer legitimate for that case, exactly the honest check 3 insists on before trusting any of these numbers.

Key relation — lumped capacitance

\[ \frac{T-T_\infty}{T_i-T_\infty}=e^{-t/\tau}, \qquad \tau=\frac{\rho V c}{hA}, \qquad Bi=\frac{hL_c}{k}<0.1 \]

Check \(Bi\) before trusting \(\tau\) — the formula does not warn you when it stops applying.

Problem 1 of 11 · solve, then check

The quenched pin, checked and timed

Problem. A 12 mm-diameter steel pin (\(k=45\ \text{W/mK}\), \(\rho=7850\ \text{kg/m}^3\), \(c=470\ \text{J/kgK}\)) is quenched in oil at \(h=750\ \text{W/m}^2\text{K}\). Check the Biot number, then find \(\tau\) and the time to reach 5% of the initial excess.

Solution. \(L_c=r/2=3.0\ \text{mm}\), \(Bi=hL_c/k=0.05<0.1\): lumped is valid. \(\tau=\rho L_c c/h=14.76\ \text{s}\). \(t_{5\%}=\tau\ln(20)=44.2\ \text{s}\).

Problem 2 of 11 · solve, then check

The same pin, quenched in water instead

Problem. The identical pin is quenched in water, \(h=5000\ \text{W/m}^2\text{K}\), instead of oil. Check whether lumped capacitance is still legitimate.

Solution. \(Bi=h L_c/k=5000\times0.0030/45=0.33\) — over the 0.1 threshold. Lumped capacitance is no longer a legitimate model for this case; a one-term or chart-based solution (2) is required instead.

Problem 3 of 11 · solve, then check

Doubling the pin's radius

Problem. A second pin, identical material and quench (\(h=750\ \text{W/m}^2\text{K}\)), has twice the diameter (24 mm instead of 12 mm). Find its Biot number and time constant, and compare \(\tau\) to the original pin's.

Solution. \(L_c=r/2=6\ \text{mm}\), \(Bi=hL_c/k=0.10\) — right at the 0.1 boundary, lumped capacitance is only just still defensible. \(\tau=\rho L_c c/h=29.52\ \text{s}\), exactly \(2\times\) the original 14.76 s — \(\tau\) scales linearly with \(L_c=V/A\), not with its square as Chvorinov's rule does.

Problem 4 of 11 · multiple choice

The Biot number \(Bi=hL_c/k\) is best understood as a ratio of which two quantities?

\(Bi=(L_c/k)/(1/h)\) — internal conduction resistance in the numerator, external convection resistance in the denominator.

Problem 5 of 11 · multiple choice

A body's Biot number is 0.6. Which model should be used for its transient response?

\(Bi=0.6\) is well above the \(0.1\) rule-of-thumb line; internal conduction resistance is not negligible, so lumped capacitance is not a legitimate shortcut here.

Problem 6 of 11 · multiple choice

Near the surface of a suddenly-heated semi-infinite solid, the surface heat flux behaves as time proceeds:

As the affected depth grows, the local temperature gradient at the surface relaxes, and the semi-infinite solid solution gives a surface flux falling as \(1/\sqrt{t}\).

Problem 7 of 11 · multiple choice

Both this lesson's lumped time constant and casting's Chvorinov's rule are governed by the same geometric ratio. Which one?

Both \(\tau=\rho Vc/(hA)\) and Chvorinov's \(t\propto(V/A)^2\) are built on \(V/A\) — but with different exponents, per 3.

Problem 8 of 11 · multiple choice

Why does Chvorinov's rule use \((V/A)^2\) while this lesson's lumped \(\tau\) uses \(V/A\) to the first power?

Lumped capacitance is externally (convection) limited, linear in V/A; Chvorinov's solidifying shell is internally (conduction) limited, which is what pushes the exponent to two — a real physical distinction, not a coincidence.

Problem 9 of 11 · multiple choice

In this lesson's worked pin, what does reaching \(t=\tau\) specifically correspond to?

By definition of the exponential solution, one time constant is the time for the excess to fall to \(1/e\) of its starting value — visible directly on this lesson's diagram.

Problem 10 of 11 · multiple choice

Doubling a lumped body's characteristic length \(L_c\) (same material, same h) changes \(Bi\) and \(\tau\) how?

Both \(Bi=hL_c/k\) and \(\tau=\rho L_c c/h\) are directly proportional to \(L_c\); doubling \(L_c\) doubles both, exactly as this lesson's third solve problem shows numerically.

Problem 11 of 11 · multiple choice

A one-term (Heisler-chart-style) solution is appropriate once which condition is met?

Once \(Fo\gtrsim0.2\), the series solution is dominated by its first term, and the one-term/chart approximation becomes accurate enough for engineering use.

Applied case — factory floor (Kuwait)

A technician estimating quench or preheat duration for a batch of small parts checks the Biot number before reaching for the lumped-capacitance formula at all — per 1 and 4, the same part can sit comfortably under the \(Bi<0.1\) line in one quench medium and clear it entirely in a more aggressive one, and applying the easy formula regardless of which medium is in use would misestimate the time by a real, checkable margin.

An engineer estimating how long a hot component in a shut-down process line takes to reach a safe-to-touch temperature uses the same time-constant idea, once \(Bi\) is confirmed small, as a first-pass lockout-duration estimate — a legitimate, quick engineering number precisely because the check in 1 was not skipped first.
