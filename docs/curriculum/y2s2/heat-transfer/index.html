HTX 253 · YEAR 2 · SEMESTER 2

Heat Transfer

Conduction, convection, radiation, and the exchangers that trade them — with the transient conduction that casting already used, and the fouling economics every process plant argues about.

What you'll learn

✓Conduction, convection, and radiation rate equations side by side.
✓The fin equation derived and solved; efficiency and effectiveness.
✓Lumped capacitance with its Biot-number entry ticket, then the semi-infinite solid and one-term chart solutions.
✓Boundary layers and the Nusselt–Reynolds–Prandtl grammar.
✓Developing versus developed flow; constant-flux and constant-temperature results.
✓Grashof and Rayleigh numbers; vertical plates and enclosures.

Syllabus

01

Mechanisms and the thermal circuit

Conduction, convection, and radiation rate equations side by side. Thermal resistance networks for walls and cylinders. Insulation economics and the critical-radius surprise.

Taught from — Çengel & Ghajar, Ch. 1–3; video: LearnChemE (CU Boulder) — heat transfer

Checkpoint questions
  • Series thermal resistances: which layer dominates and why?
  • What does the critical insulation radius warn about thin pipes?
02

Steady conduction and fins

The fin equation derived and solved; efficiency and effectiveness. Finned surfaces as area multipliers on air-cooled equipment. When fins stop paying.

Taught from — Çengel & Ghajar, Ch. 3; video: LearnChemE (CU Boulder) — heat transfer

Checkpoint questions
  • Why do fins have diminishing returns with length?
  • Fin efficiency versus effectiveness: what does each measure?
03

Transient conductionCore 60 · in production

Lumped capacitance with its Biot-number entry ticket, then the semi-infinite solid and one-term chart solutions. Heating and quench times estimated honestly. The physics already met in Chvorinov's rule, now in its own right.

Taught from — CORE 60 · B5 · Çengel & Ghajar, Ch. 4; video: LearnChemE (CU Boulder) — heat transfer

Checkpoint questions
  • Biot number 0.05 for a quenched pin: is lumped capacitance legitimate, and what does τ = ρVc/hA mean physically?
  • Why does the surface heat flux into a semi-infinite solid fall as 1/√t?
  • Roughly how does doubling a plate's thickness change its one-term-model cooling time?
04

Forced convection: external

Boundary layers and the Nusselt–Reynolds–Prandtl grammar. Flat plates, cylinders, and tube banks by correlation. Using correlations without believing them past their range.

Taught from — Çengel & Ghajar, Ch. 7; video: LearnChemE (CU Boulder) — heat transfer

Checkpoint questions
  • What do Nusselt, Reynolds, and Prandtl each encode?
  • Why does a correlation's validity range matter?
05

Forced convection: internal

Developing versus developed flow; constant-flux and constant-temperature results. The Dittus–Boelter workhorse with its fine print. Heat pickup in real piping runs.

Taught from — Çengel & Ghajar, Ch. 8; video: LearnChemE (CU Boulder) — heat transfer

Checkpoint questions
  • Constant flux versus constant wall temperature: which internal-flow result applies to a steam-jacketed pipe?
  • What does 'fully developed' mean thermally?
06

Natural convection

Grashof and Rayleigh numbers; vertical plates and enclosures. Hot cabinets and un-fanned motors in Gulf ambient. Why still summer air is an equipment rating problem.

Taught from — Çengel & Ghajar, Ch. 9; video: LearnChemE (CU Boulder) — heat transfer

Checkpoint questions
  • Why does natural convection depend on orientation?
  • What does Rayleigh number decide?
07

Boiling and condensation

The boiling curve and critical heat flux respected properly. Film condensation on tubes. The phase-change coefficients that make steam equipment compact.

Taught from — Çengel & Ghajar, Ch. 10; video: LearnChemE (CU Boulder) — heat transfer

Checkpoint questions
  • Why is critical heat flux a safety limit, not a performance target?
  • Film versus dropwise condensation: which do real plants get?
08

Heat exchangersCore 60 · in production

Shell-and-tube and plate architectures; overall coefficient built from resistances, fouling included. LMTD design and effectiveness-NTU rating methods, each derived, each worked once. Fouling monitoring as the maintenance engineer's stake in this lesson.

Taught from — CORE 60 · B6 · Çengel & Ghajar, Ch. 11; video: LearnChemE (CU Boulder) — heat transfer

Checkpoint questions
  • Counterflow exchanger: hot stream 150→90 °C, cold 30→70 °C. Compute the LMTD.
  • Fouling resistance doubles: what happens to U and to the outlet temperatures, qualitatively?
  • When is effectiveness-NTU the only honest method, and why does LMTD fail there?
09

Radiation: fundamentals and exchange

Blackbody laws, emissivity, and view factors in simple geometries; two-surface enclosures and network-method basics. Radiation shields, and combined convection–radiation surface losses. Estimating bare-pipe losses the way an energy audit does — the physics the furnace and the thermographer's camera share.

Taught from — Çengel & Ghajar, Ch. 12–13; video: LearnChemE (CU Boulder) — heat transfer

Checkpoint questions
  • Why does emissivity dominate radiation exchange for gray surfaces?
  • Estimate bare-pipe loss: which two modes add, and when does radiation win?
10

Mass transfer analogy

Diffusion and convective mass transfer via the heat-transfer analogy. Evaporation and drying estimates. The shared mathematics made explicit.

Taught from — Çengel & Ghajar, Ch. 14; video: LearnChemE (CU Boulder) — heat transfer

Checkpoint questions
  • State the heat–mass transfer analogy in one sentence.
  • Why does evaporation cool the liquid left behind?
11

Thermal design mini-project

Rate or size a small exchanger against a duty with fouling margins, then critique the design's failure modes. Calculations packaged for review. The heat-transfer counterpart of the shaft project.

Taught from — Çengel & Ghajar, Ch. 11; video: LearnChemE (CU Boulder) — heat transfer

Checkpoint questions
  • In the exchanger mini-project, which resistance dominated, and what would you attack first?
  • What fouling margin did you carry, and how does it show on the datasheet?
  • Çengel & Ghajar, Heat and Mass Transfer: Fundamentals and Applications (McGraw-Hill)
