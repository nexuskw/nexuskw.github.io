MET 204 · YEAR 2 · SEMESTER 2

Metrology & Quality Control

Measurement you can defend: instruments, uncertainty, calibration, GD&T verification, and the SPC toolkit — the quality half of a production engineer's day job.

Syllabus

01

Measurement fundamentals

Accuracy, precision, resolution, and bias untangled for good. Traceability chains to national standards. The vocabulary that makes calibration certificates readable.

Taught from — NPTEL: Metrology

Checkpoint questions
  • Accuracy versus precision: sketch the four target patterns.
  • What does traceability connect your caliper to?
02

Dimensional instruments

Calipers, micrometers, dial indicators, gauge blocks, and comparators — use, care, and abuse. Abbe error and why instrument geometry matters. Temperature effects at the micron level.

Taught from — NPTEL: Metrology

Checkpoint questions
  • Why does Abbe error make a caliper worse than a micrometer?
  • What does 20 °C have to do with a certified gauge block?
03

Uncertainty and calibration

Uncertainty budgets built from type A and B components. Calibration intervals and out-of-tolerance investigations. A micrometer calibration worked end to end.

Taught from — NPTEL: Metrology

Checkpoint questions
  • Type A versus Type B uncertainty: what distinguishes them?
  • Out-of-tolerance found at calibration: what must the investigation cover?
04

Form, fits, and GD&T verification

Measuring flatness, roundness, and runout — the rotating-equipment repair set. Verifying position with datums. CMM measurement in outline.

Taught from — NPTEL: Metrology; Groover inspection chapters

Checkpoint questions
  • Measure runout on a shaft: setup, instrument, and what the number means.
  • What does a datum system add to a position check?
05

Limit gauging and Taylor's principle

Limits and fits recalled from the drawing course, then verified the shop-floor way: GO/NO-GO limit gauges designed with gauge-maker's tolerance. Taylor's principle — why the GO gauge must check the whole feature while the NO-GO checks point by point. Where attribute gauging beats measurement, and where it hides trouble.

Taught from — NPTEL: Metrology

Checkpoint questions
  • Why must a GO gauge check the whole feature while the NO-GO checks point by point?
  • Gauge-maker's tolerance sits inside the work tolerance: who does that protect?
06

Surface texture measurement

Ra, Rz, and the stylus trace read properly. Cutoffs and filtering honestly explained. Finish specs matched to gaskets, seals, and bearing seats.

Taught from — NPTEL: Metrology

Checkpoint questions
  • Why does cutoff length change a measured Ra?
  • Match a finish spec to a mechanical-seal face versus a painted guard.
07

Statistical process control I

Variation: common versus special cause — the distinction all of SPC rests on. X̄–R charts built and read. Control limits versus specification limits, never confused again.

Taught from — Groover, quality chapters; NPTEL

Checkpoint questions
  • Common versus special cause: why does the response differ?
  • Why are control limits never the same thing as spec limits?
08

Statistical process control II and capability

Attribute charts in brief; capability indices Cp and Cpk computed and abused-proofed. What Cpk = 1.33 actually promises. Capability studies as pre-production gatekeeping.

Taught from — Groover, quality chapters; NPTEL

Checkpoint questions
  • Cp = 1.8 but Cpk = 0.7: what is the process doing?
  • What does Cpk = 1.33 promise in defect terms, roughly?
09

Acceptance sampling

Sampling plans, OC curves, and the risks each party carries. AQL semantics without the folklore. Where sampling makes sense and where 100% inspection is owed.

Taught from — Groover, quality chapters

Checkpoint questions
  • What do producer's and consumer's risk mean on an OC curve?
  • When is 100% inspection actually owed?
10

Gauge R&R and measurement systems

Repeatability and reproducibility studies: can the gauge see the tolerance? %GRR computed and judged. The study that should precede every SPC deployment and rarely does.

Taught from — NPTEL: Metrology

Checkpoint questions
  • %GRR at 35%: what does that verdict mean for your SPC plans?
  • Repeatability versus reproducibility: whose variation is each?
11

Quality systems and audits

ISO 9001 logic: say it, do it, prove it. Nonconformance, CAPA, and audit trails. Where quality systems touch maintenance records — more often than either side admits.

Taught from — NPTEL: Metrology and Quality Control

Checkpoint questions
  • Say-do-prove: map ISO 9001's logic onto a calibration program.
  • Where do maintenance records enter a quality audit?

  • Groover, inspection & quality chapters
