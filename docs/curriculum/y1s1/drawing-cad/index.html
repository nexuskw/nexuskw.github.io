DRW 102Year 1 · Semester 1

Engineering Drawing & CAD

The plant's native written language: orthographic projection, sections, dimensions and tolerances, weld and surface symbols — read first, draw second — plus enough parametric CAD to model and detail real parts.

11 lessons · 0 in the Core 60 · Primary texts:

  • MIT OCW / NPTEL: Engineering Drawing and Computer Graphics (nptel.ac.in)
01

Why drawings rule the plant

The drawing as a contract between design, fabrication, and maintenance. Title blocks, revisions, and drawing control. What 'as-built' means and why it is often untrue.

Taught from — NPTEL: Engineering Drawing

02

Projection theory and orthographic views

First-angle and third-angle projection and how to tell them apart on sight. Choosing principal views. Reading a three-view drawing back into a 3-D object.

Taught from — NPTEL: Engineering Drawing

03

Isometric and pictorial views

Isometric drawing for communication with the shop floor. Sketching a part freehand in proportion. When a pictorial view misleads and a section is owed instead.

Taught from — NPTEL: Engineering Drawing

04

Sections and conventions

Full, half, offset, and revolved sections. Hatching conventions by material. The conventions for ribs, shafts, and fasteners that are deliberately not sectioned.

Taught from — NPTEL: Engineering Drawing

05

Dimensioning practice

Datum thinking: dimension from function, not convenience. Chain versus baseline dimensioning and tolerance stack-up. Over-dimensioning as an error, not a courtesy.

Taught from — NPTEL: Engineering Drawing

06

Tolerances, fits, and limits

Limits and fits for shafts and holes; clearance, transition, and interference classes. Why a bearing seat tolerance is a maintenance issue, not a design nicety. Reading fit callouts on real drawings.

Taught from — NPTEL: Engineering Drawing

07

GD&T essentials

Feature control frames, datums, flatness, position, runout. What GD&T buys over coordinate tolerances. Runout callouts as the language of rotating-equipment repair standards.

Taught from — NPTEL: Engineering Drawing

08

Fasteners, welds, and surface symbols

Thread designation and fastener callouts. Weld symbols on structural drawings — the arrow side convention that everyone gets wrong once. Surface finish symbols and Ra values.

Taught from — NPTEL: Engineering Drawing

09

Parametric CAD: parts

Sketch–constrain–feature workflow in any parametric CAD tool. Design intent: modeling so the part edits sanely. Fully defined sketches as a discipline.

Taught from — NPTEL: Engineering Drawing and Computer Graphics

10

Parametric CAD: assemblies and drawings

Mates and degrees of freedom in assemblies. Generating dimensioned drawings from models. Bills of materials and balloon callouts — the bridge to spare-parts thinking.

Taught from — NPTEL: Engineering Drawing and Computer Graphics

11

Reading plant drawings

GA drawings, fabrication drawings, and datasheets read side by side. Tracing a dimension from a pump GA to a foundation bolt plan. Practice on structural-steel and equipment drawings.

Taught from — NPTEL: Engineering Drawing
