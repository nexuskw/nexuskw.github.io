Career outlook — where this course pays

Scripting is the quietest promotion lever in engineering: every plant runs on exported data, and the engineer who turns exports into answers becomes the one the morning meeting waits for — in any sector:

  • Infrastructure & utilities — refinery and utility engineers at operators such as KNPC live downstream of data historians: a scripted pull of tag data, cleaned and reduced to availability and excursion counts, replaces an afternoon of spreadsheet surgery — and this course's cleaning, KPI, and plotting lessons are exactly that pipeline.
  • Specialized manufacturing — production and maintenance engineers at regional manufacturers such as Kirby track OEE, scrap, and downtime by cause; the Pareto ranking and honest-denominator habits of Lesson 11 are the monthly review, automated and rerunnable.
  • Construction & EPC — project-controls and commissioning engineers at global contractors such as Bechtel reconcile progress exports, punch-list dumps, and test-pack data; the read-raw-never-edit discipline is precisely what audit-heavy project environments demand of their numbers.

Interview angle: the winning story is concrete: "here is a spreadsheet task I replaced with a script — same input, same numbers every run, an assertion that catches the bad rows." Reproducibility, told as a story, separates a data-literate engineer from a spreadsheet operator — and this course hands you that story.
