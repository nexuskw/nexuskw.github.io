STA 103 · YEAR 1 · SEMESTER 1

Engineering Statics

Equilibrium as a habit of mind: forces, moments, free-body diagrams, trusses and frames, friction, and centroids — the grammar under every strength-of-materials and machine-design calculation that follows.

Forces, vectors, and resultantsFull lesson

Force as a vector; components and resultants in 2-D and 3-D. Units and the weight–mass distinction that still catches graduates. Resolving cable and strut forces.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • Why is weight not mass, and where does the confusion bite in bolt-load calculations?
  • Resolve a 10 kN cable force at 30° to horizontal into components.

Moments and couplesFull lesson

After this lesson you can answer:
  • What does a couple do that a single force cannot?
  • State Varignon's theorem and one situation where it saves work.

The free-body diagramFull lesson

The single most important skill in this course: isolate, replace, label. Support types and their reactions. Every equilibrium error traces back to a bad FBD.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • Name the three steps of a free-body diagram and the most common omission.
  • What reactions does a fixed support supply that a pin does not?
