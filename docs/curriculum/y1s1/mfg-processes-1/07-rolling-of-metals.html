Rolling of metals

Lecture

After this lesson you can answer:
  • With μ = 0.25 and 350 mm radius rolls, what is the maximum draft in a single pass?
  • Compute the contact length for Δh = 6 mm on 300 mm radius rolls.
  • Rolling needs the friction that other forming processes fight — why?

Objectives

  1. Check whether a pass is possible — the friction bite condition — before computing anything else.
  2. Estimate roll force, torque, and power for a flat-rolling pass with units carried through.
  3. Distinguish hot from cold rolling by purpose, and map common rolled-product defects to their stand-level causes.

Key equations — sourced (Groover, Ch. 19)

Flat rolling: draft limit, contact length, force, torque, power
dmax = μ2 R(1)
L = √R (h0 − hf) ,   F = Ȳf · w · L ,   T = 0.5 F L ,   P = 2π N F L(2)

Draft d = h₀ − hf cannot exceed μ²R or the rolls skid instead of biting — friction is what drives the stock through. L is the projected contact length, Ȳf the mean flow stress over the pass, w the width, N the roll speed (rev/time; P covers both rolls).

Condensed worked example — hot rolling a steel flat

h₀ = 30 mm → hf = 22 mm, R = 300 mm, w = 250 mm, μ = 0.30 (hot), Ȳf ≈ 180 MPa (representative hot flow stress), N = 50 rpm:

bite: d = 8 mm ≤ μ²R = 0.09 × 300 = 27 mm ✓
L = √(300 × 8) = 49 mm
F = 180×10⁶ × 0.250 × 0.049 = 2.2 MN  (≈ 225 tonnes-force on the stand)
T = 0.5 × 2.2×10⁶ × 0.049 ≈ 54 kN·m per roll
P = 2π × (50/60) × 2.2×10⁶ × 0.049 ≈ 566 kW

Misconceptions

"Friction is the enemy in rolling, like in every forming process."
In rolling, friction is the engine: equation (1) says no friction, no bite, no process. The cost is that the same friction raises force and wear. Hot rolling runs rough and grippy on purpose; cold rolling lubricates and accepts smaller drafts.
"Cold rolling is just hot rolling with tighter tolerance."
Cold rolling changes the product's properties: strain hardening raises strength, and the surface arrives clean and precise. Hot rolling buys big shape change cheaply above recrystallization; cold rolling sells strength and finish. Different products, not different settings.

Defect map

Wavy edges and center buckle — roll bending making thickness (and hence elongation) uneven across the width; crown and camber management is the fix. Alligatoring — center-line weakness splitting the bar at exit (upstream casting quality returns: Lesson 04's centerline segregation). Rolled-in scale and seams — descaling practice and cast-billet surface condition. In a rebar mill, ribs and grade marks add roll-wear as a first-class quality variable.

Kuwait floor

Kuwait Steel's mill takes the caster's billets through a reheat furnace and a train of stands to deformed rebar. Every equation above is live there: bite limits set pass schedules, stand forces size the mill's motors and bearings — the same bearings and gearboxes the reliability lessons of Year 4 monitor.
