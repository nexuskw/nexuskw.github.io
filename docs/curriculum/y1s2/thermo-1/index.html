THM 155Year 1 · Semester 2

Thermodynamics I

Energy accounting with consequences: properties, the first law for closed systems and control volumes, the second law, and entropy — taught toward boilers, compressors, and turbines rather than abstract cycles.

11 lessons · 0 in the Core 60 · Primary texts:

  • Çengel (with Boles), Thermodynamics: An Engineering Approach (McGraw-Hill) — via MIT OCW/NPTEL equivalents
  • NPTEL: Basic Thermodynamics (nptel.ac.in)
01

Systems, properties, and state

Closed and open systems; intensive and extensive properties; equilibrium and process paths. The state postulate in working form. Units and sign conventions fixed once, forever.

Taught from — NPTEL: Basic Thermodynamics

02

Pure substance properties and steam tables

Phase regions, quality, and the P–v and T–s diagrams. Steam-table fluency drills — the skill utilities engineering assumes. Superheat and subcooling located physically.

Taught from — NPTEL: Basic Thermodynamics

03

Work and heat

Boundary work computed for standard processes; heat as energy in transit, not a substance. Path dependence demonstrated. Reading work from a P–v diagram's area.

Taught from — NPTEL: Basic Thermodynamics

04

First law: closed systems

Energy balance for closed systems with internal energy and enthalpy distinguished. Specific heats and ideal-gas relations. Piston-cylinder and rigid-tank problems solved in a fixed format.

Taught from — NPTEL: Basic Thermodynamics

05

First law: control volumes

Steady-flow energy equation term by term. Nozzles, turbines, compressors, pumps, throttles, and heat exchangers as one-line devices. The equation this pathway will use weekly for four years.

Taught from — NPTEL: Basic Thermodynamics

06

Transient flow processes

Charging and discharging tanks; the uniform-state, uniform-flow model. Compressed-air receiver behavior as the working example. Where steady-flow assumptions break.

Taught from — NPTEL: Basic Thermodynamics

07

Second law and machines

Kelvin–Planck and Clausius statements; thermal efficiency and COP defined honestly. Carnot limits as ceilings no vendor brochure escapes. Refrigerators and heat pumps as reversed engines.

Taught from — NPTEL: Basic Thermodynamics

08

Entropy

Entropy as a property; the increase principle for isolated systems. T–s diagrams and isentropic reference processes. Entropy generation as the bookkeeping of irreversibility.

Taught from — NPTEL: Basic Thermodynamics

09

Isentropic efficiency

Turbine, compressor, and pump efficiencies defined against the isentropic ideal. Real machine performance located on the h–s diagram. The number that separates a healthy compressor from a worn one.

Taught from — NPTEL: Basic Thermodynamics

10

Exergy first contact

Available work and dead states in outline. Exergy destruction as the cost of irreversibility. Why utilities audits speak this language — a preview of Thermo II.

Taught from — NPTEL: Basic Thermodynamics

11

Gas power cycle sketches

Otto, Diesel, and Brayton cycles in ideal form. Mean effective pressure and back-work ratio. Enough cycle literacy to hold a conversation about gas turbines — depth deferred to Thermo II.

Taught from — NPTEL: Basic Thermodynamics
