Theory of metal machining

Lecture

Orthogonal cutting model: shear plane, chip formation, cutting forces, and the Merchant relation. Cutting temperature and where tool wear comes from. Taylor tool-life equation with its exponents read as economics.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • Cutting at 200 m/min gives 30 min of tool life; with Taylor exponent n = 0.25, evaluate the constant C = vTⁿ.
  • Where does most of the cutting energy end up, and why does that cap cutting speed?
  • If tool–chip friction falls, what does the Merchant relation predict for the shear angle and cutting force?
