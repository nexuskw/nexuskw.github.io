FLD 203Year 2 · Semester 1

Fluid Mechanics

Pressure, flow, and loss: statics to Bernoulli to real pipe networks with pumps attached — taught so that a pump curve, a system curve, and an NPSH margin become native reading.

12 lessons · 1 in the Core 60 · Primary texts:

  • Çengel & Cimbala, Fluid Mechanics: Fundamentals and Applications (McGraw-Hill)
01

Fluid properties

Density, viscosity, vapor pressure, surface tension — each tied to a plant consequence. Newtonian behavior and where slurries and polymers depart from it. Vapor pressure flagged early: cavitation is coming.

Taught from — Çengel & Cimbala, Ch. 2

02

Pressure and fluid statics

Pressure fields, manometry, and forces on submerged surfaces. Gauge versus absolute pressure — the eternal instrumentation trap. Tank and dam wall calculations done cleanly.

Taught from — Çengel & Cimbala, Ch. 3

03

Flow description and Reynolds number

Streamlines, flow regimes, and the Reynolds number as the master dimensionless group. Laminar and turbulent flow distinguished physically. Where transition sits in real pipe sizes.

Taught from — Çengel & Cimbala, Ch. 4, 8

04

Mass conservation and continuity

Control-volume mass balances, steady and unsteady. Velocity–area trades in ducts and nozzles. The one-line equation that gating design already used in MFG 105.

Taught from — Çengel & Cimbala, Ch. 5

05

Bernoulli, energy equation, pipe flow, and pump curvesCore 60 · guide queued

Bernoulli derived with its assumptions on the table, then upgraded to the energy equation with head loss and pump head. Major losses via friction factor and the Moody chart; minor losses by coefficient. System curve meets pump curve — the operating point found, the lesson this whole course exists to deliver.

Taught from — CORE 60 · B7 · Çengel & Cimbala, Ch. 5, 8, 14

06

Momentum analysis

Control-volume momentum for forces on bends, nozzles, and vanes. Thrust blocks and pipe anchor loads. Why elbows in large lines need restraint calculations.

Taught from — Çengel & Cimbala, Ch. 6

07

Dimensional analysis and similarity

Buckingham Pi with engineering judgment. Model testing and scale-up logic. The affinity-law preview: why pump performance scales the way it does.

Taught from — Çengel & Cimbala, Ch. 7

08

Laminar and turbulent pipe flow in depth

Fully developed laminar flow derived to the Hagen–Poiseuille result. Turbulent velocity profiles and roughness effects. Entrance lengths and where gauges should not be installed.

Taught from — Çengel & Cimbala, Ch. 8

09

Piping networks and valves

Series and parallel lines; three-reservoir style problems by iteration. Valve types and their loss and control characters. Reading a pump-and-piping isometric as a resistance network.

Taught from — Çengel & Cimbala, Ch. 8

10

Flow measurement

Orifice, venturi, rotameter, magnetic, ultrasonic, and Coriolis meters — principle, accuracy, and installation effects. Differential-pressure metering derived from Bernoulli. Straight-run requirements as physics, not superstition.

Taught from — Çengel & Cimbala, Ch. 8; instrumentation sections

11

Turbomachinery fundamentals

Euler turbomachine equation derived from angular momentum. Velocity triangles for a centrifugal impeller. Specific speed as the shape parameter of the pump world.

Taught from — Çengel & Cimbala, Ch. 14

12

Compressible flow first contact

Mach number, choking, and converging-nozzle behavior. Relief and blowdown implications. Enough gas dynamics to respect what happens downstream of a PSV.

Taught from — Çengel & Cimbala, compressible flow chapter
