Electronics & Sensors

From signal to decision: analog conditioning, digital basics, and the industrial sensor families — temperature, pressure, flow, level, proximity, vibration — wired, scaled, and sanity-checked on a production line.

Syllabus

01

Semiconductors, diodes, and power supplies

Diode behavior and rectification; regulated DC supplies that feed every control panel. Ripple and regulation specs read from a datasheet. The 24 VDC world introduced.

Taught from — NPTEL: Basic Electronics

Checkpoint questions
  • What does a rectifier-plus-regulator chain deliver, and what ripple means?
  • Why is 24 VDC the control-panel standard?
02

Transistors and switching

The transistor as a switch; driving relays and indicator loads. Saturation, cutoff, and heat. Solid-state relays versus electromechanical ones.

Taught from — NPTEL: Basic Electronics

Checkpoint questions
  • Transistor as a switch: what do saturation and cutoff correspond to?
  • When does a solid-state relay beat an electromechanical one?
03

Op-amps and signal conditioning

Amplification, filtering, and instrumentation amplifiers for small sensor signals. Common-mode rejection as noise defense. Why thermocouple microvolts survive a noisy plant at all.

Taught from — NPTEL: Basic Electronics

Checkpoint questions
  • Why does a thermocouple signal need an instrumentation amplifier?
  • What does common-mode rejection actually reject?
04

Digital logic and interfaces

Logic levels, gates, and simple combinational thinking. Discrete inputs and outputs as the PLC sees them. Sinking versus sourcing — the wiring confusion resolved once.

Taught from — NPTEL: Basic Electronics

Checkpoint questions
  • Sinking versus sourcing I/O: what actually differs in the wiring?
  • Why do PLCs read logic levels, not analog values, on discrete inputs?
05

Sensors on a production lineCore 60 · in production

The industrial sensor families in one working tour — RTD/thermocouple, pressure transmitters, flow and level, proximity and photoelectric — each with its signal, failure modes, and wiring. The 4–20 mA loop derived as an engineering decision, live zero included. A filling line's sensor set walked end to end.

Taught from — CORE 60 · G2 · NPTEL: Industrial Instrumentation alternative (enable captions)

Checkpoint questions
  • A 4–20 mA transmitter spans 0–5 m of level and reads 12 mA: what is the level?
  • Why is the live zero at 4 mA instead of 0 mA?
  • RTD or thermocouple for a 90 °C bearing housing: which, and on what grounds?
06

Temperature measurement in depth

RTD classes and self-heating; thermocouple types, cold junctions, and extension wire sins. Thermowells and response lag. Choosing and installing temperature points that tell the truth.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Instrumentation alternative (enable captions)

Checkpoint questions
  • Why does an RTD self-heat, and how is it managed?
  • What does a thermowell cost you in response time?
07

Pressure and level measurement in depth

Transmitter anatomy: sensing, isolation, and impulse lines. DP level measurement with wet legs — where the arithmetic goes wrong. Purges, seals, and Gulf-summer condensation traps.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Instrumentation alternative (enable captions)

Checkpoint questions
  • Wet leg versus dry leg in DP level: what changes in the arithmetic?
  • Why do impulse lines fail more than transmitters?
08

Vibration and speed sensors

Piezoelectric accelerometers with IEPE powering; mounting and its frequency ceiling, previewed for the condition-monitoring course. Eddy-current proximity probes for shaft motion on journal bearings. Tachometers and encoders — the speed and phase references every machinery diagnosis leans on.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Instrumentation alternative (enable captions)

Checkpoint questions
  • Why does an accelerometer's mounting method set its usable frequency range?
  • What does a proximity probe measure that a case-mounted accelerometer cannot?
09

Analog-to-digital and sampling

Sampling, aliasing, and resolution in bits. Anti-alias filtering as a non-negotiable. Why a vibration channel's sample rate sets what defects it can see — the data-collector connection.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Instrumentation alternative (enable captions)

Checkpoint questions
  • State the sampling theorem and one aliasing symptom on a trend.
  • What does bit resolution mean for a 4–20 mA channel?
10

Signal transmission and noise

Grounding, shielding, and segregation of signal from power. Ground loops diagnosed and killed. HART over 4–20 mA in outline.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Instrumentation alternative (enable captions)

Checkpoint questions
  • What causes a ground loop, and what breaks it?
  • Why route signal and power cables apart?
11

Sensor selection and installation project

Specify the measurement points for a small process skid: variables, technologies, ranges, and installation details. Datasheets filled honestly. The exercise instrument engineers call 'doing the index'.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Instrumentation alternative (enable captions)

Checkpoint questions
  • What belongs on an instrument datasheet before purchase?
  • Pick measurement points for a small skid: what logic orders the list?
