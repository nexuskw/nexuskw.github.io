Electronics & Sensors

Career outlook — where this course pays

Electronics & Sensors is the reason the rest of the plant has numbers to control by — the sector picks which numbers:

  • Analog and mixed-signal semiconductors — component engineers at manufacturers such as Texas Instruments design the op-amp, regulator, and instrumentation-amplifier building blocks behind Lessons 1 and 3 — the same rectify/filter/regulate chain and CMRR-driven signal conditioning this course develops from first principles.
  • Precision sensors and data-acquisition ICs — applications engineers at manufacturers such as Analog Devices build the MEMS accelerometers and ADCs at the center of Lessons 8 and 9 — the mounting-resonance trade-off and the \( F_s>2f_{max} \) Nyquist criterion are exactly the specifications printed on those datasheets.
  • Industrial sensors and discrete automation components — applications engineers at manufacturers such as Omron build the proximity sensors and discrete I/O devices of Lesson 4 — the sourcing/sinking mismatch this course flags as the most common discrete-I/O wiring error is a routine technical-support call for them.

Interview angle: the classic probe is a diagnostic scenario — "this signal is noisy or wrong, walk me through how you would isolate the cause" — expecting a systematic elimination across wiring convention (sourcing/sinking), common-mode versus differential noise (CMRR and shielding), grounding (ground loops), and sampling (Nyquist and aliasing), rather than a guess at a single cause.
