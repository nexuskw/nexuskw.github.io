LSS 405 · YEAR 4 · SEMESTER 1

Lean & Six Sigma

Waste and variation hunted systematically: value-stream thinking, flow and pull, SMED, and the DMAIC toolkit — the production engineer's improvement operating system, statistics included.

01

Lean's logic

Value versus waste; the seven wastes spotted on a real line walk. Flow interrupted as the master symptom. Lean as engineering, stripped of ceremony.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • Name the seven wastes with one maintenance-shop example each.
  • Why is flow interruption the master symptom?

Taught from — NPTEL operations management content

02

Value stream mapping

Current-state mapping with data boxes and lead-time ladders. Future-state design rules. The map as an argument for specific, dated changes.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • What belongs in a data box on a current-state map?
  • Why does the lead-time ladder embarrass most processes?

Taught from — NPTEL operations management content

03

Flow, pull, and kanban

Batch-size effects derived from Little's law; supermarkets and kanban loops sized. Push versus pull honestly compared. Where pull fails: long changeovers and unstable processes.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • What does Little's law say about batch size and lead time?
  • When does pull genuinely fail?

Taught from — NPTEL operations management content

04

SMED: changeover reduction

Internal versus external setup separated, then converted. Video-study method. Changeover time as capacity: the filling-line flavor change worked as the example.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • Internal versus external setup: give two conversions on a filling line.
  • Why does changeover time equal capacity?

Taught from — NPTEL operations management content

05

5S and visual management

Workplace organization as failure prevention, not housekeeping theater. Visual controls that surface abnormality in seconds. The maintenance-shop 5S case: tools, spares, and shadow boards.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • What does 5S prevent, mechanically, in a maintenance shop?
  • What makes a visual control actually visual?

Taught from — NPTEL operations management content

06

DMAIC: Define and Measure

Problem statements with baselines and scope; measurement-system checks before data is trusted — gauge R&R recalled. Process capability as the Measure phase's verdict. Projects chartered so they can fail honestly.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • What must a charter's baseline contain before Measure starts?
  • Why does gauge R&R precede data collection?

Taught from — NPTEL quality content; STS 206 foundations

07

DMAIC: Analyze

Root-cause candidates from fishbones and stratification; hypothesis tests separating signal from noise. Regression on process data. The statistics course earning its keep.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • Stratify downtime by shift and product: what pattern would implicate process, not people?
  • Which test compares two shifts' means honestly?

Taught from — NPTEL quality content; STS 206 foundations

08

DMAIC: Improve and Control

Solutions tested by experiment where stakes justify it; error-proofing over exhortation. Control plans and response plans that outlive the project team. The handover that decides whether gains persist.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • Error-proofing versus retraining: why does one persist?
  • What does a control plan hand to the process owner?

Taught from — NPTEL quality content

09

Design of experiments in practice

Two-level factorials run on a process; effects and interactions computed and plotted. Blocking and randomization as insurance. A sealing-parameter experiment worked end to end.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • Design a 2² experiment on sealing: factors, response, and blocking.
  • What does randomization insure against?

Taught from — NPTEL quality content; STS 206

10

Lean maintenance

Wrench-time study as the maintenance value-stream map; travel, waiting, and parts-chasing quantified. Kitting and planning as waste removal. The improvement lens turned on the maintenance function itself.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • What does a wrench-time study typically find, and what absorbs the hours?
  • Which lean tool attacks parts-chasing directly?

Taught from — SMRP framework; NPTEL operations content

11

Improvement project simulation

A guided DMAIC on a provided dataset: charter to control plan with tollgate reviews. Statistical tools used where they earn their place. The portfolio artifact for interviews.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • In the simulation, which tollgate nearly killed the project, and why?
  • What survived to the control plan?
