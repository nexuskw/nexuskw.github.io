CDM 403 · YEAR 4 · SEMESTER 1

Condition Monitoring & Predictive Maintenance

Reading machines before they fail: PdM strategy on P–F economics, vibration analysis from sensor to spectrum diagnosis, thermography, oil analysis, and ultrasound — all eight Core-60 lessons, the vibration analyst's foundation.

  • NPTEL: Mechanical Vibrations (prerequisite); ISO 18436-aligned practice content
  • SMRP CMRP framework
01

PdM strategy and P–F economicsCore 60 · guide queued

Condition monitoring justified from the P–F curve: detection windows, inspection intervals at a fraction of P–F, and the money mathematics of caught-early versus run-to-wreck. Technology-to-failure-mode mapping as the program design act. The strategy lesson that keeps PdM from becoming gadget collecting.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • A failure mode has a 4-month P–F interval: make the economic case for a monthly route.
  • Match technologies to failure modes: bearing spall, misalignment, hot electrical joint, degraded oil.
  • Why is owning an analyzer not the same as having a PdM program? Name the missing pieces.

Taught from — CORE 60 · D1 · SMRP CMRP framework; video: Mobius Institute Watch lectures ▶

02

Vibration fundamentals for analystsCore 60 · guide queued

Amplitude, frequency, and phase as the analyst's three currencies; displacement, velocity, and acceleration with their frequency emphases and unit conversions. Overall levels and ISO-style zone thinking versus spectral analysis. The vibration course's theory recast into measurement language.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • Convert 4 mm/s RMS at 100 Hz to a displacement amplitude — order of magnitude.
  • Why velocity for general severity, acceleration for bearings and gears, displacement for low-speed machines?
  • What does a commissioning baseline buy that absolute ISO-zone limits cannot?

Taught from — CORE 60 · D2 · NPTEL: Mechanical Vibrations; ISO 18436-aligned content; video: Mobius Institute — vibration Watch lectures ▶ In Arabic — alternative

03

Measurement and the FFTCore 60 · guide queued

Accelerometer mounting and its frequency ceiling; Fmax, resolution, averaging, and windowing chosen deliberately. The FFT's gifts and traps — leakage, aliasing already met in Electronics. A measurement setup specified for a motor-pump train as the condensed example.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • With Fmax 1,000 Hz and 1,600 lines, what is the spectral resolution per line?
  • Why does a loose magnet mount silently cap your usable Fmax?
  • What does a Hanning window trade away, and what does it buy?

Taught from — CORE 60 · D3 · NPTEL: Mechanical Vibrations; signal content from ELX 205; video: Mobius Institute — vibration Watch lectures ▶ In Arabic — alternative

04

Spectrum analysis I: unbalance, misalignment, loosenessCore 60 · guide queued

The first diagnostic trio: 1× dominant radial for unbalance; 2× and axial content for misalignment; harmonic families and raised floors for looseness. Phase analysis as the disambiguator. Case spectra read line by line, the way the certification practicals do.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • Dominant 1× radial, little axial, stable phase: unbalance or misalignment — and what confirms it?
  • A harmonic forest 1×–10× over a raised floor: which fault family, and what mechanical check follows?
  • Why is phase measured before any field balancing attempt?

Taught from — CORE 60 · D4 · ISO 18436-aligned content; NPTEL: Mechanical Vibrations; video: Mobius Institute — vibration Watch lectures ▶ In Arabic — alternative

05

Spectrum analysis II: bearings and gearsCore 60 · guide queued

Rolling-element defect frequencies computed from geometry; the enveloping/demodulation technique and why early bearing damage hides from ordinary spectra. Gear mesh frequencies and sidebands as the gearbox's confession. Severity staging from first detection to removal decision.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • BPFO factor 3.58 at 1,480 rpm: what frequency line do you hunt?
  • Why does early bearing damage appear in enveloped spectra long before ordinary velocity spectra?
  • In a gearbox spectrum, sideband spacing around GMF identifies what?

Taught from — CORE 60 · D5 · ISO 18436-aligned content; video: Mobius Institute — vibration Watch lectures ▶ In Arabic — alternative

06

Infrared thermographyCore 60 · guide queued

Radiation physics from the heat-transfer course made practical: emissivity, reflection, and why shiny metal lies to the camera. Electrical and mechanical survey practice; delta-T severity conventions. The condensed example: an MCC survey with three findings graded and reported.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • A shiny bus bar with emissivity ~0.2 reads 45 °C: why might it be far hotter, and what fixes the reading?
  • Why must electrical thermography surveys run under meaningful load?
  • One phase of a three-phase connection runs 25 K hotter than its siblings: severity logic?

Taught from — CORE 60 · D6 · Çengel & Ghajar, Ch. 12 foundations; ISO 18436-aligned practice; video: Mobius Institute Watch lectures ▶

07

Oil analysis and wear debrisCore 60 · guide queued

The three questions of oil analysis — fluid health, contamination, machine wear — with the tests behind each: viscosity, TAN, particle counts, elements, ferrography in outline. Sampling technique as the program's real quality gate. Reading a gearbox report against its history.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • Viscosity up 20% and TAN doubled: what has happened to the oil, and what typically drove it?
  • Iron and chromium rising together in a gearbox sample: what does the pairing suggest?
  • Why does the sampling point decide whether an oil-analysis trend means anything?

Taught from — CORE 60 · D7 · SMRP framework; tribology content from MNT 306; video: Mobius Institute Watch lectures ▶

08

Ultrasound, MCA, and building routesCore 60 · guide queued

Airborne and structure-borne ultrasound for leaks, bearings, and electrical faults; motor current analysis in outline for rotor-bar and load diagnostics. Building PdM routes: what, where, how often, and who closes the loop. The program-assembly lesson that turns four technologies into one system.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • What makes a 40 kHz ultrasound sensor find leaks your ears cannot?
  • In motor current analysis, broken rotor bars appear as sidebands spaced by what quantity?
  • Name three consistency disciplines that keep a monthly route's data comparable.

Taught from — CORE 60 · D8 · SMRP CMRP framework; video: Mobius Institute Watch lectures ▶

09

Online monitoring and protection systems

Permanently installed sensors, protection versus prediction, and trip logic on critical trains. Proximity probes on journal-bearing machines. When online systems earn their cost.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • Protection versus prediction systems: what does each shutdown philosophy imply?
  • Why do journal-bearing machines carry proximity probes rather than accelerometers?

Taught from — ISO 18436-aligned content; video: Mobius Institute Watch lectures ▶

10

Diagnostics case marathon

A dozen anonymized cases across the four technologies read cold, then debriefed. Differential diagnosis practice: competing explanations ranked. The pattern library a working analyst carries.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • In the case marathon, which two cases shared symptoms but not causes?
  • What does differential diagnosis discipline prevent?

Taught from — Course synthesis; video: Mobius Institute Watch lectures ▶

11

PdM data management and trending

Baselines, alarm limits, and trend curation; false-alarm discipline. Integrating PdM findings into the CMMS work flow. The administrative half that makes the technical half matter.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • Why do alarm limits need curation as machines age?
  • What closes the loop between a PdM finding and the CMMS?

Taught from — SMRP framework; video: Mobius Institute Watch lectures ▶

12

PdM program design project

For a given equipment list: criticality-based technology assignment, routes, intervals, and KPI set, costed and staffed. The deliverable a reliability engineer presents to justify the program. Course capstone.

After this lesson you can answer:
  • Which criticality tier got online monitoring in your design, and why?
  • How did you cost-justify the route frequencies?
