Syllabus

01

Centrifugal pump theory and curvesCore 60 · in production

Euler head to real head: the H–Q curve derived, then read like an instrument — BEP, shutoff, runout, and efficiency contours. Power and affinity laws with their honest limits. The condensed example: re-rating a pump for a speed change without leaving the curve's validity.

Taught from — CORE 60 · E1 · Çengel & Cimbala, Ch. 14; supplementary video: The Efficient Engineer — pumps In Arabic — alternative

Checkpoint questions
  • Affinity laws: speed drops 10% — what happens to flow, head, and power?
  • Where on the H–Q curve does efficiency peak, and what goes wrong running far out on the curve?
  • Why is sustained operation at shutoff destructive when a brief excursion is survivable?
02

System curves, NPSH, and cavitationCore 60 · in production

System resistance built from static head plus friction; the operating point as intersection. NPSH available derived from the installation, NPSH required read from the vendor, and the margin between them defended. Cavitation's physics, sound, and damage signature — and why throttling a suction valve is a firing offense.

Taught from — CORE 60 · E2 · Çengel & Cimbala, Ch. 8, 14; supplementary video: The Efficient Engineer — pumps In Arabic — alternative

Checkpoint questions
  • NPSHa = 6 m against NPSHr = 4.5 m: verdict, and what margin does good practice want?
  • Why is a 5 m suction lift of 60 °C water already in trouble before any calculation?
  • Slightly throttling the discharge quiets cavitation noise: explain the mechanism.
03

Positive displacement pumpsCore 60 · in production

Gear, screw, diaphragm, and reciprocating pumps: flow nearly independent of pressure, and the relief-valve consequence that follows. Pulsation and its dampeners. Where PD pumps replace centrifugals — viscosity, metering, and low-flow/high-head duty.

Taught from — CORE 60 · E3 · NPTEL: pump-related content; Çengel & Cimbala

Checkpoint questions
  • Why must a PD pump never start against a closed discharge without a relief path?
  • At fixed speed, what changes gear-pump delivery as pressure rises, and by what mechanism?
  • Why does metering duty default to PD pumps over centrifugals?
04

CompressorsCore 60 · in production

Centrifugal and reciprocating compressors with the thermodynamics attached: polytropic work, discharge temperature, and capacity control. Surge — the phenomenon, the map, and the anti-surge loop. Air and process compression duties across the six anchor plants.

Taught from — CORE 60 · E4 · NPTEL: Applied Thermodynamics content; Çengel & Cimbala

Checkpoint questions
  • What sets reciprocating-compressor discharge temperature, and what limits pressure ratio per stage?
  • Define surge in a centrifugal compressor in one sentence.
  • Why does intercooling between stages save power? Tie it to the T–s picture.
05

Mechanical sealsCore 60 · in production

Seal anatomy: faces, secondary seals, springs, and the fluid film that does the real work. Seal plans in outline — flush, quench, and dual-seal logic. Failure reading: heat checking, coking, dry running. The single highest-value component lesson for a process-plant maintenance engineer.

Taught from — CORE 60 · E5 · SMRP framework; NPTEL rotating machinery content; supplementary video: The Efficient Engineer

Checkpoint questions
  • Name a mechanical seal's two sealing interfaces — which rotates and which is static?
  • Why can thirty seconds of dry running end a seal that survived three years?
  • What does a flush plan physically deliver to the seal faces, and against which failure modes?
06

Bearings and lubrication in serviceCore 60 · in production

Rolling-element and journal bearings from the design course, now in service: fits, internal clearance, lubrication regimes, and the L10 promise versus installed reality. Grease quantity and interval arithmetic. Failure atlas: spalling, fluting, false brinelling — each traced to its cause.

Taught from — CORE 60 · E6 · NPTEL: Machine Design content; SMRP framework; supplementary video: The Efficient Engineer

Checkpoint questions
  • L10 is 40,000 h at design load; load rises 26%. Estimate the new L10 for ball bearings.
  • Why is over-greasing a bearing as damaging as starvation?
  • Fluting on a bearing raceway: cause, and which drive technology made it epidemic?
07

Couplings, alignment, and balancingCore 60 · in production

Coupling types and what each forgives; shaft alignment by rim-and-face and reverse-dial logic with tolerances stated in real units; field balancing in one and two planes. Thermal growth targets. The precision-maintenance trio that removes the two commonest vibration sources before they start.

Taught from — CORE 60 · E7 · NPTEL: Theory of Machines content; SMRP framework

Checkpoint questions
  • What do rim-and-face and reverse-dial methods both actually measure between two shafts?
  • Give the physical mechanism that puts misalignment's signature at 2× running speed.
  • Between balance grades G6.3 and G2.5, what changes physically on the rotor?
08

Gearboxes and their failure modesCore 60 · in production

Industrial gearbox architectures; loads and life from the design course compressed into inspection practice. The failure atlas: micropitting, macropitting, scuffing, tooth bending fatigue — with the oil-analysis and vibration signatures of each. Backlash and contact-pattern checks at rebuild.

Taught from — CORE 60 · E8 · NPTEL: Machine Design content; SMRP framework

Checkpoint questions
  • Compute gear-mesh frequency for a 23-tooth pinion at 1,475 rpm.
  • Micropitting versus macropitting: which lubrication property is implicated first, and why?
  • Sidebands around GMF spaced at output-shaft speed: what are they telling you?
09

Fans, blowers, and turbines

Fan laws and curves; static versus total pressure; balance and belt issues as the routine failure diet of cooling and ventilation duty in Gulf summer. Steam and gas turbines in outline: stage energy conversion recalled from thermodynamics, governing and protection systems, and steam quality's blade consequences. The rest of the rotating fleet, respected from a maintenance distance.

Taught from — Çengel & Cimbala, Ch. 14; NPTEL: Applied Thermodynamics

Checkpoint questions
  • State the fan laws and what a 10% speed cut buys in power.
  • What does overspeed protection interrupt, and how fast must it act?
10

Machinery installation and commissioning

Foundations, grouting, and soft foot; piping strain as the silent misalignment source. Commissioning checks and baseline data capture. The installation quality that decides the first five years of vibration history.

Taught from — SMRP framework

Checkpoint questions
  • What does soft foot do to a freshly aligned machine?
  • Why does piping strain masquerade as misalignment?
11

Rotating equipment failure investigation project

A staged failed-pump scenario: history, vibration data, oil results, and teardown photographs provided. Produce the failure analysis and corrective actions. The course's eight core lessons deployed as one investigation.

Taught from — SMRP framework; NPTEL reliability content

Checkpoint questions
  • In the failure scenario, which evidence stream contradicted the first hypothesis?
  • What corrective action addressed cause rather than symptom?
  • Çengel & Cimbala, Ch. 14 (turbomachinery)
  • NPTEL: Turbomachinery and maintenance-related courses (nptel.ac.in)
  • SMRP CMRP framework for practice content
