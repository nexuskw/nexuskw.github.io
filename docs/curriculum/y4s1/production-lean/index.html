PPL 405 · YEAR 4 · SEMESTER 1

Production Planning & Lean Six Sigma

Running and improving the production system in one course: capacity, master scheduling, MRP/ERP, and shop-floor scheduling from the planning tradition; value streams, flow, SMED, 5S, and DMAIC from the lean and Six Sigma tradition.

Syllabus

01

Production systems and layouts

Job shop, batch, line, and continuous production with their planning personalities. Layout types and material flow. Where each Kuwait anchor plant sits on this spectrum.

Taught from — Groover, production systems chapters

Checkpoint questions
  • Place the six anchor plants on the job-shop-to-continuous spectrum.
  • What does layout type do to material travel?
02

Capacity and takt

Design versus effective capacity; utilization and efficiency separated. Takt time computed and confronted with cycle times. Bottleneck identification as the first act of scheduling.

Taught from — NPTEL: Production and Operations Management

Checkpoint questions
  • Compute takt for 4,800 units in two 8-h shifts.
  • Why does the bottleneck define capacity regardless of averages?
03

Aggregate planning and MPS

Level versus chase strategies costed. The master production schedule as the plant's promise ledger. Frozen zones and why they exist.

Taught from — NPTEL: Production and Operations Management

Checkpoint questions
  • Level versus chase: what does each cost in a dairy's demand cycle?
  • What does a frozen zone protect?
04

MRP and ERP logic

Bills of materials, lead-time offsetting, and the MRP explosion worked by hand once. ERP as MRP grown up. Data accuracy as the system's oxygen.

Taught from — NPTEL: Production and Operations Management

Checkpoint questions
  • Explode a two-level BOM by hand: what offsets apply?
  • Why does inventory accuracy gate MRP's usefulness?
05

Shop-floor scheduling

Dispatching rules and their trade-offs; Gantt logic and finite capacity. Sequence-dependent setups — the changeover matrix. Why the schedule is always wrong and still necessary.

Taught from — NPTEL: Production and Operations Management

Checkpoint questions
  • Compare SPT and EDD dispatching on lateness.
  • Why do sequence-dependent setups wreck naive schedules?
06

Lean's logic

Value versus waste; the seven wastes spotted on a real line walk. Flow interrupted as the master symptom. Lean as engineering, stripped of ceremony.

Taught from — NPTEL operations management content

Checkpoint questions
  • Name the seven wastes with one maintenance-shop example each.
  • Why is flow interruption the master symptom?
07

Value stream mapping, flow, and pull

Current-state mapping with data boxes and lead-time ladders. Future-state design rules. The map as an argument for specific, dated changes. Batch-size effects derived from Little's law; supermarkets and kanban loops sized. Push versus pull honestly compared. Where pull fails: long changeovers and unstable processes.

Taught from — NPTEL operations management content

Checkpoint questions
  • What belongs in a data box on a current-state map?
  • What does Little's law say about batch size and lead time?
08

SMED, 5S, and visual management

Internal versus external setup separated, then converted. Video-study method. Changeover time as capacity: the filling-line flavor change worked as the example. Workplace organization as failure prevention, not housekeeping theater. Visual controls that surface abnormality in seconds. The maintenance-shop 5S case: tools, spares, and shadow boards.

Taught from — NPTEL operations management content

Checkpoint questions
  • Internal versus external setup: give two conversions on a filling line.
  • What does 5S prevent, mechanically, in a maintenance shop?
09

DMAIC I: Define, Measure, Analyze

Problem statements with baselines and scope; measurement-system checks before data is trusted — gauge R&R recalled. Process capability as the Measure phase's verdict. Projects chartered so they can fail honestly. Root-cause candidates from fishbones and stratification; hypothesis tests separating signal from noise. Regression on process data. The statistics course earning its keep.

Taught from — NPTEL quality content; STS 206 foundations

Checkpoint questions
  • What must a charter's baseline contain before Measure starts?
  • Stratify downtime by shift and product: what pattern would implicate process, not people?
10

DMAIC: Improve and Control

Solutions tested by experiment where stakes justify it; error-proofing over exhortation. Control plans and response plans that outlive the project team. The handover that decides whether gains persist.

Taught from — NPTEL quality content

Checkpoint questions
  • Error-proofing versus retraining: why does one persist?
  • What does a control plan hand to the process owner?
11

Improvement project simulation

A guided DMAIC on a provided dataset: charter to control plan with tollgate reviews. Statistical tools used where they earn their place. The portfolio artifact for interviews.

Taught from — Course synthesis

Checkpoint questions
  • In the simulation, which tollgate nearly killed the project, and why?
  • What survived to the control plan?
