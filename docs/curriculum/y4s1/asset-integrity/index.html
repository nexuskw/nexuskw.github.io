Asset Integrity Management

Keeping contained energy contained: integrity management systems, risk-based inspection, fitness-for-service thinking, and the inspection programs for static equipment — the asset integrity engineer's course.

Syllabus

01

What asset integrity means

Integrity as the discipline over static equipment: vessels, piping, tanks, relief systems. Loss-of-containment consequence logic. The role's boundaries against maintenance and inspection functions.

Taught from — NPTEL reliability content; industry framework survey

Checkpoint questions
  • What equipment classes does integrity own, and what consequence unites them?
  • Where does integrity end and maintenance begin?
02

Damage mechanisms inventory

The degradation bestiary organized for static equipment: thinning, cracking, embrittlement, mechanical damage. Susceptibility mapped from process conditions. The corrosion course operationalized into an integrity register.

Taught from — Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 17

Checkpoint questions
  • Sort thinning, cracking, and embrittlement by inspectability.
  • Why does susceptibility mapping precede any inspection plan?
03

Inspection methods for static equipment

Visual, UT thickness, shear-wave, and profile radiography deployed on vessels and piping. CML selection — where to measure so the data means something. Inspection effectiveness graded honestly.

Taught from — NPTEL NDT-related content

Checkpoint questions
  • Why does CML selection decide whether thickness data means anything?
  • What does inspection effectiveness grading admit about methods?
04

Risk-based inspection

Probability and consequence of failure combined into inspection priority. Interval setting from remaining life and confidence. RBI as budget allocation defended with arithmetic.

Taught from — NPTEL reliability content

Checkpoint questions
  • What two axes drive RBI priority, and what data feeds each?
  • How does RBI defend an interval extension to a regulator?
05

Fitness for service thinking

The engineering question behind FFS: is the flawed equipment safe to run, and for how long? Corrosion allowance and minimum thickness logic; crack-like flaws flagged to specialists. When to call the assessment versus replace.

Taught from — Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 8

Checkpoint questions
  • What question does FFS answer that inspection cannot?
  • When does a wall-thinning finding demand assessment versus rerate versus replace?
06

Pressure relief system integrity

Relief philosophy: every credible overpressure case owns a device. PSV testing intervals and pre-test capture of as-found condition. The protective systems audit trail regulators read first.

Taught from — NPTEL process safety content

Checkpoint questions
  • Why must every credible overpressure case map to a device?
  • What does as-found PSV test data feed?
07

Piping integrity

Piping classes, circuits, and injection-point programs; dead legs and small-bore vulnerability. Support and vibration walkdowns. Piping as the plant's largest and least-loved asset.

Taught from — NPTEL content; Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 17

Checkpoint questions
  • Why are dead legs disproportionately dangerous?
  • What makes small-bore connections a fatigue problem?
08

Storage tank integrity

Atmospheric tank anatomy: shell, bottom, roof; settlement and corrosion patterns. Internal inspection logic and intervals. Floor scanning and the repair decisions it triggers.

Taught from — NPTEL content

Checkpoint questions
  • Why does tank bottom corrosion hide from external inspection?
  • What triggers an internal tank inspection?
09

Corrosion under insulation programs

CUI's mechanism, temperature bands, and moisture pathways. Inspection strategies from stripping to screening. The Gulf coastal plant's standing CUI battle, budgeted properly.

Taught from — Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 17

Checkpoint questions
  • Name CUI's moisture pathways and the design details that feed them.
  • Why does screening precede stripping in CUI programs?
10

Integrity data and KPIs

Thickness data management, corrosion-rate calculation, and remaining-life projections. Overdue-inspection and temporary-repair KPIs. The integrity dashboard a plant manager should demand.

Taught from — NPTEL reliability content

Checkpoint questions
  • Turn thickness history into a corrosion rate: what checks the number?
  • Which integrity KPIs would a plant manager read weekly?
11

Integrity management system project

For a small unit: damage-mechanism register, CML plan, inspection schedule, and KPI set assembled into one integrity plan. The document set of the asset integrity engineer's first 90 days.

Taught from — Course synthesis

Checkpoint questions
  • In the unit plan, which damage mechanism dominated the register?
  • What did the KPI set watch that inspection alone would miss?
  • Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 8, 17 (failure & corrosion foundations)
  • NPTEL: reliability and corrosion course content (nptel.ac.in)
