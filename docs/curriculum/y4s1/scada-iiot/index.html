SCA 404 · YEAR 4 · SEMESTER 1

SCADA, DCS & IIoT

The data layer above the machines: SCADA architecture, historians, OEE from real signals, and the industrial internet — where production data becomes the numbers management runs the plant by.

What you'll learn

✓RTUs/PLCs, servers, and clients; polling versus report-by-exception.
✓Tags, scan classes, and deadbands; compression and its analytical costs.
✓Availability, performance, and quality computed from real machine states and counts — with the state-model design that decides whether OEE means anything.
✓Alarm rationalization: priorities, floods, and standing-alarm hygiene.

Syllabus

01

SCADA architecture

RTUs/PLCs, servers, and clients; polling versus report-by-exception. Redundancy patterns. SCADA versus DCS versus PLC+HMI — the taxonomy settled.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Automation and Control alternative (enable captions)

Checkpoint questions
  • Polling versus report-by-exception: what does each cost?
  • Where does redundancy belong in a SCADA architecture?
02

Data acquisition and historians

Tags, scan classes, and deadbands; compression and its analytical costs. Time-series storage and retrieval. The historian as the plant's memory — and its garbage-in problem.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Automation and Control alternative (enable captions)

Checkpoint questions
  • What does deadband compression discard, and when does that bite analysis?
  • Why is the historian only as good as its tag metadata?
03

OEE from signalsCore 60 · in production

Availability, performance, and quality computed from real machine states and counts — with the state-model design that decides whether OEE means anything. Loss trees beneath the top number. The condensed example: an OEE calculation built from a filling line's PLC signals, honestly handling changeovers and micro-stops.

Taught from — CORE 60 · G4 · NPTEL: Industrial Automation and Control alternative (enable captions); Groover, automation chapters

Checkpoint questions
  • Availability 90%, performance 95%, quality 99%: compute OEE.
  • A 10-minute changeover is logged as running-at-zero-rate: which OEE factor lies, and how does the state model fix it?
  • Why do sub-30-second micro-stops vanish from manual logs yet dominate performance loss?
04

Alarm systems at scale

Alarm rationalization: priorities, floods, and standing-alarm hygiene. Metrics for alarm-system health. The control-room human factors this decides.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Automation and Control alternative (enable captions)

Checkpoint questions
  • What does an alarm flood do to operators, and what rationalization fixes?
  • Name two health metrics of an alarm system.
05

Industrial communication for data

OPC UA as the interoperability workhorse; MQTT for telemetry. Mapping the automation stack's data paths. Where integration projects actually stall.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Automation and Control alternative (enable captions)

Checkpoint questions
  • What does OPC UA standardize that drivers never did?
  • Why is MQTT's publish-subscribe model suited to telemetry?
06

IIoT sensors and edge devices

Wireless condition sensors, gateways, and edge processing. Battery, bandwidth, and placement realities. Retrofit monitoring for brownfield plants — the honest use case.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Automation and Control alternative (enable captions)

Checkpoint questions
  • What decides wireless sensor placement besides signal?
  • Why is retrofit monitoring the honest IIoT use case?
07

Online monitoring and protection systems

Permanently installed sensors, protection versus prediction, and trip logic on critical trains. Proximity probes on journal-bearing machines. When online systems earn their cost.

Taught from — ISO 18436-aligned content; video: Mobius Institute Watch lectures ▶

Checkpoint questions
  • Protection versus prediction systems: what does each shutdown philosophy imply?
  • Why do journal-bearing machines carry proximity probes rather than accelerometers?
08

Dashboards and reporting that change behavior

KPI design: leading versus lagging, owner versus audience. Dashboard sins: vanity metrics and unactionable charts. Production meetings run from data with provenance.

Taught from — NPTEL content; course synthesis

Checkpoint questions
  • Leading versus lagging KPIs: give one of each for maintenance.
  • What makes a dashboard change behavior rather than decorate?
09

Analytics and anomaly detection in outline

Statistical baselines and simple models on historian data; what 'AI for PdM' can and cannot promise today. Pilot design with success criteria. Skepticism as an engineering instrument.

Taught from — NPTEL content

Checkpoint questions
  • What baseline must precede any anomaly-detection pilot?
  • Name two failure modes of 'AI for PdM' projects.
10

OT/IT convergence and security

Network zones and conduits; patching and backup discipline for control systems. Remote vendor access governed. The security posture a data project must not degrade.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Automation and Control alternative (enable captions)

Checkpoint questions
  • What do zones and conduits segment, and against what?
  • Why are control-system backups tested restores, not stored files?
11

Plant data project

From a provided historian extract: build state logic, compute OEE, find the top three losses, and present the case for one improvement. Data engineering in production clothing. Course capstone.

Taught from — Course synthesis

Checkpoint questions
  • In the data project, which loss surprised you once states were honest?
  • What single improvement did the data justify?
