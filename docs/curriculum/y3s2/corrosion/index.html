COR 355Year 3 · Semester 2

Corrosion & Degradation

The Gulf's tax on steel: electrochemical fundamentals, the corrosion forms bestiary, marine and coastal service, and the defenses — materials, coatings, cathodic protection, inhibitors — that asset integrity budgets buy.

10 lessons · 1 in the Core 60 · Primary texts:

  • Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 17; NPTEL: Corrosion-related materials courses (nptel.ac.in)
01

Electrochemistry of corrosion

Anodes, cathodes, and the corrosion cell derived from half-reactions. Galvanic series versus EMF series — the practical one wins. Corrosion rate units and conversions.

Taught from — Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 17

02

Corrosion in Gulf serviceCore 60 · guide queued

Chlorides, heat, humidity, and marine atmosphere as the region's standing corrosion load. Pitting and crevice attack on stainless; CUI — corrosion under insulation — as the hidden budget-eater; seawater cooling circuits. The condensed example: ranking degradation threats for a coastal plant's asset register.

Taught from — CORE 60 · B4 · Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 17

03

Uniform, galvanic, and crevice corrosion

The 'benign' uniform mode and when it isn't; galvanic couples with area-ratio arithmetic; crevices as chemistry chambers. Design details that create or kill crevices. Gasket and fastener implications.

Taught from — Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 17

04

Pitting and localized attack

Pit initiation on passive films; chloride's special villainy. PREN as a stainless selection number. Why pits sink ships that thickness surveys called healthy.

Taught from — Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 17

05

Environmentally assisted cracking

Stress corrosion cracking's three-ingredient recipe; chloride SCC of austenitics; hydrogen embrittlement and sulfide stress cracking in outline. Why EAC failures are sudden and forensic. Materials–environment pairings memorized by the industry.

Taught from — Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 17

06

Flow-assisted and mechanical degradation

Erosion-corrosion, cavitation damage, and fretting. Velocity limits in piping specs explained. The pump-impeller damage gallery interpreted.

Taught from — Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 17

07

High-temperature degradation

Oxidation kinetics and scale protectiveness; sulfidation and fuel-ash attack in outline. Creep–corrosion interactions at furnace conditions. The melt-shop and fired-heater materials problem.

Taught from — Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 17

08

Coatings and linings

Surface preparation standards as the real quality variable; coating families and their service envelopes. Inspection: holiday testing and DFT. Coating failure modes read like weld defects.

Taught from — Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 17; NPTEL corrosion content

09

Cathodic protection and inhibitors

Sacrificial and impressed-current CP with the electrochemistry made explicit. CP surveys in outline. Inhibitors and chemical treatment where coatings can't go.

Taught from — Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 17

10

Corrosion monitoring and management

Coupons, ER probes, and thickness-monitoring locations; corrosion rates fed into remaining-life arithmetic. Corrosion management systems as documents that direct money. The bridge into Year 4's asset integrity course.

Taught from — Callister & Rethwisch, Ch. 17
