ROB 353 · YEAR 3 · SEMESTER 2

Industrial Robotics

Robots as production equipment: kinematics enough to reason, plus the practical layer — end effectors, safety systems, palletizing and welding cells, and what a robot cell demands from maintenance.

What you'll learn

✓Articulated, SCARA, delta, and cartesian architectures with their sweet spots.
✓Frames and transformations; forward kinematics in concept.
✓Servo axes, gearing, and absolute encoders; mastering and calibration procedures.
✓Grippers — pneumatic, servo, vacuum, magnetic — matched to product.

Syllabus

01

Robot anatomy and types

Articulated, SCARA, delta, and cartesian architectures with their sweet spots. Axes, envelopes, and payload ratings read from datasheets. Repeatability versus accuracy — the specification that matters.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Robotics

Checkpoint questions
  • Repeatability versus accuracy: which does a robot datasheet promise?
  • Match SCARA, delta, and articulated arms to their natural tasks.
02

Kinematics fundamentals

Frames and transformations; forward kinematics in concept. The inverse problem and singularities — why some poses are unreachable at speed. Enough mathematics to respect the controller.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Robotics

Checkpoint questions
  • What is a singularity, and why does the wrist flip near one?
  • Forward versus inverse kinematics: which is the hard direction, and why?
03

Drives, encoders, and calibration

Servo axes, gearing, and absolute encoders; mastering and calibration procedures. Backlash and its symptoms in path quality. The maintenance anatomy of an arm.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Robotics

Checkpoint questions
  • Why do absolute encoders remove homing?
  • What does mastering restore after a crash?
04

End effectors and tooling

Grippers — pneumatic, servo, vacuum, magnetic — matched to product. Tool changers and dress packs, the wear items nobody budgets. Payload and center-of-gravity arithmetic.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Robotics

Checkpoint questions
  • Why does payload include the gripper and its cable dress?
  • Vacuum versus servo gripper: pick per product and defend.
05

Programming and teaching

Jogging, frames, and point teaching; motion types and speed/approximation trade-offs. Program structure for cells with variants. Offline programming in outline.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Robotics

Checkpoint questions
  • What does motion blending (approximation) trade for cycle time?
  • Why teach frames instead of points?
06

Robot safety systems

Guarding, light curtains, and safety-rated monitored stops; collaborative modes with their real limits. Risk assessment for a cell. Safety as the cell's first design input.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Robotics

Checkpoint questions
  • What does a safety-rated monitored stop guarantee?
  • Why are collaborative speeds so low in practice?
07

Palletizing and material-handling cells

End-of-line palletizing patterns and cycle-time budgets. Conveyor tracking basics. The robot cell most likely to appear at the end of a KDD-style packaging line.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Robotics

Checkpoint questions
  • What makes palletizing patterns a solved problem — and mixed cases not?
  • Why does conveyor tracking need an encoder?
08

Machine tending and assembly cells

Robots loading machine tools: door, chuck, and cycle-start interlocks, with the machine as the cell's real master. Part presentation — trays, bowl feeders, and vision in outline — as the make-or-break engineering. Assembly tasks: insertion, torque, and the compliance problem rigid parts pose.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Robotics

Checkpoint questions
  • Why is part presentation usually harder engineering than the robot motion itself?
  • What interlocks must exist before a robot reaches into a CNC machine?
09

Robotic welding cells

Torch packages, seam paths, and weave parameters; positioners as the second robot. Consumable and TCP maintenance. Robotic arc welding on structural work of the Kirby kind.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Robotics

Checkpoint questions
  • What does a positioner add to a welding cell's reach and quality?
  • Why is TCP calibration the weld path's foundation?
10

Cell integration

The robot inside the PLC's state machine: handshakes, permissives, and fault recovery paths. Peripheral devices and fixturing. Cycle-time analysis against the line's takt.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Robotics

Checkpoint questions
  • Design the PLC–robot handshake for a jam: who recovers whom?
  • Why does fixturing rank above the robot in cell accuracy?
11

Robot maintenance and diagnostics

Preventive tasks: lubrication, belts, batteries, dress-pack inspection. Error logs and drift trends as early warnings. What a robot's reliability program looks like on paper.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Robotics

Checkpoint questions
  • Which preventive tasks keep an arm's accuracy — name three.
  • What do error logs trend before failures announce themselves?
