Mechanical Vibrations

The theory under the analyzer screen: free and forced vibration, damping, multi-degree systems, rotor basics, and isolation — taught so Year 4's condition-monitoring course lands on solid mathematics.

11 lessons · 0 in the Core 60 · Primary texts:

  • NPTEL: Mechanical Vibrations (nptel.ac.in)

01

The single degree of freedom system

Equation of motion from Newton and from energy. Natural frequency's dependence on stiffness and mass — and on nothing else. The model that explains half of all machine vibration behavior.

Taught from — NPTEL: Mechanical Vibrations

02

Damped free vibration

Viscous damping and the three response regimes. Logarithmic decrement measured from a bump test. Damping ratio as the machine's shock absorber rating.

Taught from — NPTEL: Mechanical Vibrations

03

Harmonically forced vibration

The frequency response function derived: amplitude and phase versus forcing frequency. Resonance quantified by Q. Why passing through resonance on run-up is survivable but dwelling is not.

Taught from — NPTEL: Mechanical Vibrations

04

Rotating unbalance and whirl

Unbalance forcing derived from eccentric mass; response above and below critical speed. The phase flip through resonance. First contact with rotor dynamics language.

Taught from — NPTEL: Mechanical Vibrations

05

Support motion and transmissibility

Base-excited systems and force transmissibility derived. The isolation region above √2 times natural frequency. Why soft mounts protect and when they betray.

Taught from — NPTEL: Mechanical Vibrations

06

Vibration isolation design

Isolator selection: static deflection, natural frequency, damping trade-offs. Inertia blocks and their sizing logic. A compressor installation isolated on paper.

Taught from — NPTEL: Mechanical Vibrations

07

Two degrees of freedom and mode shapes

Coupled equations, natural frequencies, and mode shapes solved via the eigenproblem promised in Math II. Beating between close modes. The concept structure for all modal analysis.

Taught from — NPTEL: Mechanical Vibrations

08

Multi-DOF and continuous systems in outline

Modal superposition as the organizing idea. Beam and shaft natural frequencies from standard results. Why long rotors have families of criticals.

Taught from — NPTEL: Mechanical Vibrations

09

Torsional vibration

Shaft-train torsional models; natural frequencies of two- and three-inertia systems. Excitation from VFDs and reciprocating machines. The invisible vibration that breaks couplings.

Taught from — NPTEL: Mechanical Vibrations

10

Measurement preview: sensors and signals

Accelerometer, velocity, and displacement measurement physics; integration between them. Mounting effects on usable frequency range. The hardware bridge to Year 4's analysis course.

Taught from — NPTEL: Mechanical Vibrations

11

Field vibration phenomena tour

Resonance, beats, looseness nonlinearity, and self-excited whirl shown as measured signatures. Each tied back to its lesson's mathematics. The course's theory validated against plant reality.
