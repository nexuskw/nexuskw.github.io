Production Planning & Control

The factory's calendar: forecasting, capacity, scheduling, inventory, and MRP — how production commitments are made and kept, and where maintenance windows must be fought for inside them.

11 lessons · 0 in the Core 60 · Primary texts:

  • NPTEL: Production and Operations Management-family courses (nptel.ac.in)
  • Groover, production systems chapters
01

Production systems and layouts

Job shop, batch, line, and continuous production with their planning personalities. Layout types and material flow. Where each Kuwait anchor plant sits on this spectrum.

Taught from — Groover, production systems chapters

02

Demand forecasting

Moving averages and exponential smoothing with error tracking. Seasonality in outline. Forecasts as inputs to be doubted, not truths.

Taught from — NPTEL: Production and Operations Management

03

Capacity and takt

Design versus effective capacity; utilization and efficiency separated. Takt time computed and confronted with cycle times. Bottleneck identification as the first act of scheduling.

Taught from — NPTEL: Production and Operations Management

04

Aggregate planning and MPS

Level versus chase strategies costed. The master production schedule as the plant's promise ledger. Frozen zones and why they exist.

Taught from — NPTEL: Production and Operations Management

05

Inventory management

EOQ derived and then handled with care; safety stock from service levels; ABC classification. Holding-cost honesty. The same mathematics that will price spare parts in Year 4.

Taught from — NPTEL: Production and Operations Management

06

MRP and ERP logic

Bills of materials, lead-time offsetting, and the MRP explosion worked by hand once. ERP as MRP grown up. Data accuracy as the system's oxygen.

Taught from — NPTEL: Production and Operations Management

07

Shop-floor scheduling

Dispatching rules and their trade-offs; Gantt logic and finite capacity. Sequence-dependent setups — the changeover matrix. Why the schedule is always wrong and still necessary.

Taught from — NPTEL: Production and Operations Management

08

Line balancing

Precedence diagrams, station assignment, and balance efficiency. Rebalancing for demand changes. The packaging-line application: staffing a KDD-style line to takt.

Taught from — Groover, assembly-line chapters

09

Maintenance in the production plan

Planned windows, opportunistic maintenance, and shutdown coordination as scheduling problems. The production–maintenance negotiation formalized. Availability commitments read from both sides of the table.

Taught from — NPTEL: Production and Operations Management; SMRP framework

10

Performance measurement

Schedule adherence, throughput, WIP, and lead time as a coherent measure set. Little's law derived and used. The metrics OEE will join in Year 4.

Taught from — NPTEL: Production and Operations Management

11

Planning case study

A multi-product line planned through one quarter: forecast, MPS, MRP run, schedule, and a maintenance-window negotiation. Deliverables in planner's formats. The course's tools used as one system.

Taught from — NPTEL: Production and Operations Management
