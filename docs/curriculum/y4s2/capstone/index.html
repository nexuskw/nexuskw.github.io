CAP 456 · YEAR 4 · SEMESTER 2

Capstone — Integrated Plant Project

One plant, every course: a semester-long integrated project that takes a described factory from asset register to reliability program, production plan, and improvement portfolio — the curriculum proven as a single working toolkit.

11 lessons0 of 11 at full depth
Start lesson 01 Full curriculum

What you'll learn

✓The project plant described: process, equipment, history, and constraints.
✓Criticality analysis across the register; maintenance strategy assigned per class with RCM logic on the top tier.
✓Technology mapping, routes, and intervals for the critical equipment; baseline campaign planned.
✓Job plans for the top ten repetitive jobs; weekly scheduling process and KPI definitions.
✓Bills of materials built for the critical equipment; stocking policies driven by criticality and the failure data from the reliability phases.
✓Damage mechanisms, CML plans, and relief-device registers for the static equipment.

Syllabus

01

The plant brief and asset register

The project plant described: process, equipment, history, and constraints. Building the functional-location hierarchy and asset register. The dataset every later phase consumes.

Taught from — Course synthesis

Checkpoint questions
  • What makes a functional-location hierarchy analyzable later?
  • Which asset data did you refuse to invent, and what did you do instead?
02

Criticality and strategy assignment

Criticality analysis across the register; maintenance strategy assigned per class with RCM logic on the top tier. Deliverable: the strategy matrix with justifications.

Taught from — REL 354/455 foundations

Checkpoint questions
  • Which criticality tier got RCM, and why not all of them?
  • Defend one strategy assignment an auditor questioned.
03

PdM program design

Technology mapping, routes, and intervals for the critical equipment; baseline campaign planned. Deliverable: the condition-monitoring program document.

Taught from — CDM 403 foundations

Checkpoint questions
  • Which technology-to-failure-mode mapping drove your routes?
  • What baseline campaign precedes alarms?
04

Planning system build

Job plans for the top ten repetitive jobs; weekly scheduling process and KPI definitions. Deliverable: the planner's handbook for this plant.

Taught from — MPL 452 foundations

Checkpoint questions
  • Which of your ten job plans has the tightest estimate, and why?
  • What KPI set did the planner's handbook define?
05

Spares and materials strategy

Bills of materials built for the critical equipment; stocking policies driven by criticality and the failure data from the reliability phases. Insurance-spare decisions documented with the arithmetic on the table. Deliverable: the spares strategy section of the dossier.

Taught from — MPL 452 / REL 455 foundations

Checkpoint questions
  • Which spares decision needs Weibull data rather than min/max history?
  • What makes an insurance spare economic despite near-zero expected demand?
06

Integrity and inspection plan

Damage mechanisms, CML plans, and relief-device registers for the static equipment. Deliverable: the integrity plan section.

Taught from — AIM 402 / PEP 453 foundations

Checkpoint questions
  • Which damage mechanism topped the static register?
  • How did relief devices enter the schedule?
07

Production plan and OEE baseline

Capacity, takt, and schedule for the product mix; OEE state model defined from available signals. Deliverable: production plan with loss-accounting design.

Taught from — PPC 356 / SCA 404 foundations

Checkpoint questions
  • Where did takt and cycle time collide in your plan?
  • What state model feeds the OEE baseline?
08

Improvement portfolio

Three costed improvement cases — one reliability, one changeover, one energy — with NPV and payback. Deliverable: the investment one-pagers.

Taught from — LSS 405 / ECO 406 foundations

Checkpoint questions
  • Which improvement case had the best NPV, and what made it credible?
  • Which case did you reject despite good optics?
09

HSE integration

Risk assessments and permit requirements woven into the job plans and shutdown scope. Deliverable: the HSE annexes that make the plans executable.

Taught from — SAF 256 / HSE 454 foundations

Checkpoint questions
  • Which job plan changed after HSE integration?
  • What isolation complexity did the shutdown scope reveal?
10

The integrated report

All phases assembled into one coherent plant-engineering dossier with an executive summary written last and read first. Internal consistency audit across sections. The document that demonstrates the whole pathway.

Taught from — Course synthesis

Checkpoint questions
  • What inconsistency did the internal audit catch?
  • Why is the executive summary written last?
11

Defense and portfolio review

The dossier defended against structured challenge: assumptions probed, trade-offs justified. Feedback folded into the final portfolio. The dress rehearsal for the interviews the career section prepares.

Taught from — Course synthesis

Checkpoint questions
  • Which assumption fell first under challenge?
  • What would you build differently in a second pass?
  • Course synthesis across the entire curriculum
