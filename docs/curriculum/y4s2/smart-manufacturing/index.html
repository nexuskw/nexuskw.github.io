Smart Manufacturing & Digital Twin

Industry 4.0 without the brochure: data architectures, digital-twin levels, simulation, and predictive analytics — evaluated with an engineer's skepticism and a maintenance career's use cases.

Industry 4.0 taxonomy

The technology cluster named and sorted: connectivity, data, analytics, autonomy. Maturity models used as maps, not scorecards. Separating what is deployed from what is marketed.

Manufacturing data architecture

From sensor to historian to lake: the data pipeline with its quality gates. Context — asset hierarchies and event framing — as the expensive part. Why analytics projects die of data preparation.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industry 4.0 courses

03

Digital twin levels

Descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, and prescriptive twins distinguished by what they can answer. Asset versus process twins. An honest inventory of what each level costs to build and keep true.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industry 4.0 courses

04

Simulation of production systems

Discrete-event simulation for lines and buffers; validation against measured cycle data. Experiments on the model instead of the plant. The packaging line simulated for a debottlenecking decision.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industry 4.0 courses

05

Predictive maintenance analytics

Feature extraction from vibration and process data; models from thresholds to learning methods in outline. Validation against labeled failures — the scarce commodity. Where analytics augments and where it hallucinates.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industry 4.0 courses

Machine vision and quality 4.0

Cameras, lighting, and defect detection on moving product. Classical vision versus learned models. False-reject economics on a high-speed line.

07

Additive and digital workflows

Scan-to-print for obsolete spares; digital part inventories. Qualification questions for load-bearing prints. The maintenance storeroom's digital future, soberly assessed.

08

Cybersecurity for smart plants

Expanded attack surface of connected equipment; segmentation and monitoring revisited at plant scale. Incident response for OT. Security as a precondition, not a feature.

Economics of digitalization

Pilot-to-scale economics; the graveyard of proofs-of-concept explained. Benefits tracked against baselines the finance department accepts. Engineering economics applied to the smartest buzzwords.

Smart manufacturing case evaluation

Evaluate a vendor's condition-monitoring-plus-analytics proposal for a representative plant: architecture, claims, gaps, and a pilot design with success criteria. The skeptical adoption skill this decade rewards.
