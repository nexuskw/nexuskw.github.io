HSE for Industrial Operations

NEBOSH-aligned depth on top of Year 2's safety course: risk management systems, process safety, occupational health, environmental control, and the safety leadership expected of engineers in Gulf plants.

10 lessons · 0 in the Core 60 · Primary texts:

  • NPTEL: Industrial Safety Engineering (nptel.ac.in); NEBOSH IGC syllabus alignment (framework, not a textbook)
01

Safety management systems

Policy, plan, do, check, act — the management-system loop applied to HSE. Leading versus lagging indicators. Why paperwork systems fail without field verification.

Taught from — NEBOSH IGC alignment; NPTEL: Industrial Safety

02

Risk assessment at system level

Suitable-and-sufficient assessment; task-based versus area-based approaches. ALARP reasoning with cost-benefit honesty. Assessment as design input, not filing.

Taught from — NEBOSH IGC alignment

03

Process safety fundamentals

Major-accident hazards distinguished from occupational ones; barriers and the Swiss-cheese picture used precisely. HAZOP participation basics for mechanical engineers. The EQUATE-scale context of process safety.

Taught from — NPTEL: Industrial Safety; NEBOSH alignment

04

Occupational health

Noise, vibration, dust, and chemical exposure with measurement basics. Hearing conservation and hand-arm vibration for maintenance trades. Health surveillance as long-horizon duty of care.

Taught from — NEBOSH IGC alignment

05

Ergonomics and manual handling

Load assessment and workplace design; the maintenance task's awkward-posture reality. Mechanical aids and job design. Musculoskeletal injury as the trade's chronic epidemic.

Taught from — NEBOSH IGC alignment

06

Environmental management

Emissions, effluents, and waste streams of industrial plants; permits and monitoring in outline. Spill prevention and response. The environmental file an integrity engineer inherits.

Taught from — NEBOSH alignment; NPTEL content

07

Emergency preparedness

Scenario planning, alarm and evacuation systems, and drills that test rather than perform. Fire systems interface with maintenance. Command structure basics for engineers on scene.

Taught from — NEBOSH IGC alignment

08

Contractor and permit interfaces at scale

Multi-employer worksites and the coordination duty; permit systems under turnaround load. Induction and competence verification. Where fabrication-yard practice meets plant rules.

Taught from — NEBOSH alignment

09

Safety culture and leadership

Culture measured by what happens when nobody watches; just-culture response to error. The engineer's daily leadership: walkdowns, questions, and stopping work. Influence without authority, practiced.

Taught from — NEBOSH IGC alignment

10

HSE case file project

Build the HSE plan for a maintenance shutdown package: risk assessments, permits, isolations, emergency arrangements, and monitoring. The integration exercise that mirrors real pre-shutdown reviews.
